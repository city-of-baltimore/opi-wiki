## About OPI

### Mission

OPI combines performance, data, design, and delivery to help Baltimore City improve services in ways residents and staff can see and feel. We are a citywide operating capability, not a strategy shop and not a technology team.

### North Star

> *Respond when necessary. Build so we do not have to respond again.*

Great government is consistent and reliable. We respond when necessary, and we build so we do not have to respond again.

### What OPI is responsible for

- Running citywide performance routines, including CitiStat, agency performance plans, and follow-up.

- Strengthening Baltimore’s data infrastructure, analytics, open data program, and responsible AI readiness.

- Operating the Innovation Lab, OPI’s citywide service improvement capability.

- Coordinating cross-agency delivery on priority issues that span departments.

- Operating the administrative, communications, and portfolio management backbone for OPI.

### How OPI is organized

OPI is led by an Executive Director who serves as the City’s Chief Data Officer and Chief Performance Officer. The office is organized into four portfolios that operate the five services above.

| **Portfolio**                  | **What it does**                                                                                                                                                    |
|--------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Director’s Office | The operating backbone: portfolio management, communications, intake, hiring and onboarding, procurement coordination, knowledge management, and quality assurance. |
| Performance       | Stewards CitiStat, agency performance plans, performance measures, and follow-up. Operates Cross-Agency Delivery as a service overlay.                              |
| Data and Analytics             | Operates the data platform, analytics products, open data, data governance, and responsible AI readiness.                                                           |
| Innovation Lab                 | Operates service design, product discovery, civic technology, AI pilots tied to operations, partnerships, and tiger teams.                                          |

## Teams

*Use the four blocks below as the Teams landing page (four tiles) and the core copy for each team detail page. Each team page should answer four questions: why the team exists, what it does, what it means for agencies, and what it means for residents.*

### Director’s Office — AdminOps

The Director’s Office keeps OPI aligned, coordinated, and understood, internally and externally, so priority work moves and sticks. We protect the team’s focus, raise the quality bar for what we publish, and make progress legible to leaders, agency partners, and the public.

### Why this team exists

OPI’s work only matters if it lands in the real world: decisions made, barriers removed, and improvements sustained. The Director’s Office is the connective tissue that turns good work into durable results, by setting the operating rhythm, supporting executive decision-making, and ensuring OPI speaks with one clear voice.

### What we do

- Run OPI’s operating cadence: portfolio reviews, decision forums, escalation pathways, and reporting.

- Support executive briefings with clear decision packets and follow-up that closes loops.

- Own storytelling and communications: plain-language updates, impact narratives, and public-facing clarity.

- Coordinate partnerships and cross-city relationships that expand capacity without creating chaos.

- Maintain quality gates for public artifacts, including plain language, accessibility, and appropriate handling of sensitive information.

### What it means for agencies

A consistent, predictable partner: clear intake, clear priorities, and clarity about what is in scope. When a decision is needed, we frame it. When commitments are made, we track them to closure.

### What it means for residents

Transparency and follow-through. We publish what we can, explain what we are doing and why, and make it easier to see whether government is improving.

### Performance

Performance runs Baltimore’s performance operating system. We help City agencies set clear goals, track what matters, and turn data into action, through structured CitiStat sessions and relentless follow-through.

### Why this team exists

When services feel unreliable, residents lose time and trust, and frontline teams inherit the frustration. This team exists to make performance visible, focus leadership attention on the right problems, and drive practical improvements that agencies can sustain.

### What we do

- Run CitiStat: disciplined session preparation, facilitation, and follow-up that closes commitments.

- Support performance planning: clear outcomes, targets, and measures tied to City priorities.

- Strengthen measurement: clear metric definitions, consistent reporting, and fewer debates about what the numbers mean.

- Use fieldwork to validate reality on the ground, so decisions match what residents and staff experience.

- Escalate systemic blockers and trigger Cross-Agency Delivery support when problems require coordinated action.

### What it means for agencies

A routine that helps you lead: clear priorities, fewer surprises, and a structured space to remove roadblocks. CitiStat is not about “gotcha” accountability. It is practical problem-solving paired with follow-through after the meeting ends.

### What it means for residents

More reliable services and clearer transparency. CitiStat is designed to help agencies improve delivery in ways people can see, like cleaner streets, safer neighborhoods, and fewer unresolved service requests.

### Data and Analytics

Data and Analytics makes City data reliable, usable, and decision-ready. We build shared data products, publish open data responsibly through Open Baltimore, and prepare Baltimore for responsible use of analytics and AI.

### Why this team exists

Good decisions require trusted information. This team exists to reduce friction for residents trying to get help and for staff trying to deliver, by strengthening the data foundations that government runs on.

### What we do

- Build and maintain shared data products that support performance routines and operational decisions.

- Advance citywide analytics: turn questions into clear answers, then help agencies act on them.

- Operate the Open Baltimore program with clear documentation, refresh discipline, and a feedback loop.

- Develop data governance practices: dataset owners, definitions, quality controls, and privacy workflows.

- Prepare Baltimore for responsible AI use, with evaluation, guardrails, and human oversight.

### What it means for agencies

Fewer one-off asks and more reusable building blocks: shared datasets, clearer reporting, and analytics that reduce manual work. We strengthen agency capacity, so solutions do not collapse after launch.

### What it means for residents

Clearer information and easier-to-use public data. Better visibility into what the City is doing and how it is performing.

### Why this team exists

Cities improve when more people can diagnose problems, design solutions, and manage delivery. The Innovation Lab brings service design, product discovery, civic technology, AI applied to real operations, and strategic partnerships to City service problems, in partnership with agencies and frontline staff.

### What we do

- Service design and user research with residents, frontline staff, and agency partners.

- Digital products for agency operations: workflow tools, field data collection, status lookups, internal portals.

A partner who works alongside you to understand a service problem, test better solutions, and build tools that make the work easier. We design with agencies and frontline staff, not for them.

### What it means for residents

Services that are clearer, easier to use, and more responsive. Improvements you can see and feel.

## Programs

*Programs are the named, repeatable systems OPI runs year after year. Use these blocks for the Programs landing page and each program detail page.*

### CitiStat

CitiStat is Baltimore’s accountability program that uses data to improve how City services work for residents. It brings agency leaders together to identify problems, remove roadblocks, and track performance with consistent follow-through.

### Why it exists

CitiStat creates a regular, disciplined moment where leaders confront real performance, make decisions, and follow through, so improvement becomes routine, not occasional.

### How it works

- Each Stat runs on a predictable cadence: a clear pre-read, an in-person session focused on problem-solving, and a written follow-up memo that documents commitments.

- Sessions are guided by four tenets: accurate and timely intelligence shared by all; rapid deployment of resources; effective tactics and strategies; and relentless follow-up and assessment.

- Between sessions, OPI tracks commitments, escalates blockers, and helps agencies sustain what works.

- Cross-agency issues activate Cross-Agency Delivery instead of lingering as unresolved follow-ups.

### The 19 Stats

Baltimore runs 19 monthly Stats covering operations, public safety, community services, internal foundations, and place-based priorities. They include 311 Stat, Tech Stat, Capital Stat, Clean Stat, DGS Stat, Housing Stat, DOT Stat, Downtown Stat, Finance Stat, Fire and EMS Stat, Health Stat, HR and Labor Stat, Permit Stat, Police Stat, Rec Stat, Solid Waste Stat, Water Stat, Workplace Safety Stat, and Youth Stat.

### Where to see results

- Annual CitiStat reporting on performance, barriers, and follow-up priorities.

- Plain-language Stat impact briefs.

- Public dashboards where data is reliable and ready for publication.

### Authority

Baltimore City Code authorizes the CitiStat Program. OPI runs the method; agencies and Deputy Mayors own operational results.

### Open Baltimore

Open Baltimore is the City’s open data program and public data portal. It makes publishable City data available in open formats so residents, researchers, and partners can learn, build, and hold government accountable.

### Why it exists

Open data is trust infrastructure. When public information is timely, documented, and easy to use, it helps residents understand their city, helps agencies coordinate, and strengthens accountability.

### How it works

- City agencies publish datasets to the Open Baltimore portal in open formats, with documentation and a refresh cadence where possible.

- Data is reviewed to safeguard protected and sensitive information and to ensure it is appropriate for public release.

- OPI maintains the publishing standards and supports agency data owners.

- The program encourages feedback to improve dataset usefulness over time.

### Authority

Baltimore City Code defines the Open Data Program and the City’s responsibility to maintain the Open Baltimore portal and publish high-quality, updated data while safeguarding protected and sensitive information.

### Data Fellows

The Data Fellows Program embeds data experts into City agencies to improve government efficiency and service delivery. It strengthens agency capacity while giving early-career analysts a structured growth environment inside local government.

### Why it exists

Cities move faster when data capacity lives close to the work. Data Fellows help agencies turn everyday questions into actionable insight and build a habit of using data to improve delivery.

### How it works

- Fellows work with host agencies on priority questions and practical analytics projects.

- OPI provides structure, peer support, and professional development.

- The program builds a pipeline of data talent that stays in City government over time.

*OPI launched the program in 2019 and has expanded its reach across agencies over multiple cohorts.*

### Innovation Lab program portfolio

The Innovation Lab’s public-facing portfolio includes service design and product work the Lab is currently leading. Examples include 311 service request workflow improvements, permitting process and applicant communication, field data collection tools, parking enforcement operational products, AI-assisted resident communication, and tiger teams on priority service problems. The portfolio refreshes as projects ship and new ones begin.

## Services

*Services describe how OPI supports agencies. Each service page answers four questions: what it is, when to use it, what you can expect, and how to start.*

### Citywide Performance Management

Citywide Performance Management is the City’s discipline for setting priorities, measuring what matters, and improving over time. OPI supports agencies with performance planning and runs CitiStat routines that turn data into action.

### When to use this service

- You need clearer goals and measures for a service or priority initiative.

- You want a predictable review cadence that drives decisions and removes blockers.

- Performance is stalled and you need focused leadership attention and accountability.

### What you can expect from OPI

- Performance planning support: outcomes, targets, and measures aligned to priorities.

- A disciplined cadence through CitiStat: pre-reads, sessions, and documented follow-up.

- Support to improve metric definitions and reduce reporting friction.

- Escalation pathways when issues require decisions or cross-agency coordination.

### How we work

- We separate people from problems: high accountability, non-punitive tone.

- We use evidence, not anecdotes, and validate in the field when needed.

- We close loops: every commitment has an owner, a due date, and a definition of done.

### What success looks like

Fewer chronic service issues, faster decisions, higher closure rates on commitments, and measurable improvements in priority service outcomes, paired with public-facing transparency where appropriate.

### Citywide Data and Analytics

Citywide Data and Analytics makes City data more reliable, more usable, and more decision-ready. We help agencies answer practical questions, build shared data products, and publish open data responsibly through Open Baltimore.

### When to use this service

- You need insight to make a decision, allocate resources, or diagnose a service problem.

- You need a shared dataset or dashboard that multiple teams can rely on.

- You are preparing for CitiStat and need consistent, defensible metrics.

- You want to publish or improve an open dataset on Open Baltimore.

### What you can expect from OPI

- Decision-ready analysis: clear findings, plain-language recommendations, and defined confidence levels.

- Shared data products and documentation that reduce duplicate work.

- Support for open data governance and publishing with appropriate safeguards.

- Practical guidance on data standards and measurement definitions.

### How we work

- Start with the decision: what will change if we learn something new?

- Document assumptions and limitations so leaders can act responsibly.

- Build once for many: prioritize reusable products over one-off asks.

### What success looks like

Leaders cite the same numbers, agencies spend less time reconciling spreadsheets, open data is more timely and understandable, and analytics work leads to concrete operational changes, not just reports.

### Innovation Lab

The Innovation Lab is OPI’s citywide service improvement capability. It uses human-centered design, product management, civic technology, AI innovation, and partnerships to help agencies design, test, and implement practical improvements to City services.

### When to use this service

- A service is confusing, slow, or generating repeat contacts and needs a redesign.

- A workflow needs better tools, clearer status, or a simpler form.

- You want a small, evaluated test before building a full solution.

- A priority problem needs a tiger team to map the service, prototype solutions, and document what works.

### What you can expect from OPI

- Discovery and user research with residents and frontline staff.

- Service design, process mapping, and operational prototypes.

- Digital tools that fit how the work actually happens.

- AI pilots tied to real operational use cases, with evaluation and human oversight.

- Sustainment plans, so tools and improvements live beyond the project.

### How we work

- We design with residents, frontline staff, and agencies, not for them.

- We ship in small increments and learn in real conditions.

- We plan for ownership and maintenance from day one.

### What success looks like

Residents complete key tasks faster with fewer errors. Staff spend less time correcting forms and answering repeat questions. Improvements are sustained and measurable.

### Cross-Agency Delivery

Cross-Agency Delivery is how the City tackles problems that do not fit neatly inside one department. We convene the right leaders, define decision rights, run a disciplined delivery cadence, and remove blockers until results land.

### When to use this service

- A priority initiative requires multiple agencies to act in sequence.

- A service problem is chronic and needs a time-boxed sprint to diagnose, test fixes, and scale what works.

- Work is stalled due to unclear ownership, unclear decisions, or competing priorities.

### What you can expect from OPI

- A clear charter, decision rights, and success measures that everyone agrees on.

- Weekly delivery rhythm: milestones, blockers, and decisions needed, kept tight and action-oriented.

- Rapid fieldwork and prototyping where needed (tiger team model).

- A sustainment plan that hands ownership back to the right place with a monitoring routine.

### What success looks like

Cross-agency work moves faster with fewer surprises, barriers get resolved at the right level, and agencies inherit a sustainment plan, so improvements stick beyond the sprint.

### AdminOps

AdminOps is the internal backbone that keeps the work moving: portfolio management, quality assurance, hiring and onboarding support, procurement coordination, and the systems that turn plans into delivery.

### When to use this service

- You need help setting up a project correctly: charter, tracker, artifacts, cadence.

- You are producing a public-facing report or briefing and want QA support.

- You need help routing procurement, staffing, or operational dependencies.

- You want consistent onboarding and playbooks for new hires or partner teams.

### What you can expect from OPI

- Portfolio visibility: what is in flight, what is at risk, what needs decisions.

- Templates, checklists, and QA gates that increase consistency and reduce rework.

- Operational support for hiring, onboarding, and internal training.

- Documentation discipline: decisions and commitments captured and easy to find.

### What success looks like

Less time lost to confusion and rework, cleaner handoffs, faster onboarding, fewer surprises in delivery, and consistently high-quality public artifacts.

## How to work with OPI

OPI uses a single front door for new work. The intake form is short and routes new requests to the right team, with a definition of done that fits the size of the work.

**Quick request.** A small ask routed within five business days. Examples: a one-page brief, a small data pull, or a quick consult.

**Project.** A defined piece of work with a problem statement, owner, and approximate scope. Examples: a service redesign sprint, a dashboard, a CitiStat pre-read.

**Major engagement.** A multi-week or multi-month engagement with a charter, milestones, and a sustainment plan. Examples: a flagship digital product, a tiger team, a multi-agency delivery effort.

### Pathways by audience

- If you work for an agency: start with the intake form. We will route it to the right team and confirm scope within a week.

- If you are a partner organization (philanthropy, university, civic technology, community): contact the Director’s Office to discuss alignment with OPI priorities.

- If you are a resident: explore Open Baltimore, the Stat impact briefs, and the latest annual report. Use 311 for service requests.

- If you are a journalist or researcher: contact OPI Communications.
