## Five things every Stat must have

1. **A performance problem** — not a status update.
2. **A decision-maker in the room** — if absent, reschedule.
3. **Reliable data** — agreed sources, agreed cutoff, reviewed before the meeting.
4. **Pre-read discipline** — pre-memo and data pack out at least 48 hours ahead.
5. **Closure mechanism** — every commitment has a name, a date, and evidence of done.

## Who does what

| **Role** | **Owned by** | **Responsibility** |
|---|---|---|
| Executive owner | Executive Director, OPI | Sets the standard; brokers escalations; signs off on which agencies are in. |
| Method steward | Deputy Chief Performance Officer | Owns method, calendar, QA bar, and analyst bench. |
| Stat lead (facilitator) | OPI portfolio leader or PM | Frames the problem; runs the room; captures commitments. |
| Lead analyst | CitiStat Analyst or PM | Builds pre-memo and data pack; owns metrics; briefs the lead. |
| Inspector | CitiStat Inspector | Conducts ride-alongs, site visits, and ground-truth checks. |
| Authorizer | Deputy Mayor for the cluster | Authorizes the Stat; unblocks; reinforces decisions outside the room. |
| Agency lead | Agency director or designee | Owns agency performance and commitments. |
| Agency analyst | Agency staff | Owns agency-side data quality and the agency view. |

## Cadence at a glance

T = day of the next Stat. Days are business days.

| **Phase** | **Days** | **Owner** | **Output** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Topic intake / scoping | T−30 to T−21 | Stat lead + Method steward | Problem statement; metric set agreed. |
| Data refresh + analysis | T−20 to T−8 | Lead analyst | Updated data pack; anomalies flagged. |
| Pre-memo drafted | T−8 to T−4 | Stat lead + Lead analyst | Draft memo; questions for the room finalized. |
| Pre-read distributed | T−4 to T−2 | Stat lead | Memo + data pack to agency and authorizer. |
| Pre-brief | T−2 to T−1 | Stat lead with agency lead | Agency knows what will be asked. |
| Stat meeting | T0 | Stat lead facilitates | Decisions, commitments, next-cycle metrics. |
| Read-out + commitment log | T+1 to T+3 | Lead analyst | Commitments in tracker; authorizer briefed. |
| Mid-cycle check | T+10 to T+14 | Stat lead | Progress check; risks surfaced. |
| Closure or carry-forward | T+25 to T+30 | Stat lead + Method steward | Each commitment closed, carried, or escalated. |

## In the room (60–90 minutes)

| **Segment** | **Minutes** | **Purpose** |
|---|---|---|
| Frame | 5 | Stat lead names problem, question, desired outcome. |
| Data review | 15 | Lead analyst walks metric set and key chart; agency confirms or contests. |
| Diagnosis | 20 | Press on cause — process, capacity, technology, policy, demand. |
| Decisions and commitments | 15 | Stat lead names each commitment aloud, with owner and date; confirms agreement. |
| Carry-forward | 5 | Lead analyst reads back commitments; reconcile prior open items. |

**Facilitation norms:** press on the problem, not the person · no commitment without a name and a date · if the data is contested, agree how the dispute is resolved before the next Stat (not in the room) · if the room can't decide, the Stat lead names the escalation path and moves on.

## Escalation path

Use when a commitment cannot move, a decision is contested, or a blocker spans agencies.

1. **Stat lead** records the blocker and the decision needed in the tracker; surfaces it in the weekly radar.
2. **Method steward (DCPO)** reviews; routes to the right next routine.
3. **Authorizer (Deputy Mayor / DCA)** decides, allocates, or signals the call.
4. If the issue requires sequencing across agencies → **Cross-Agency Delivery activation** or **tiger team** charter.
5. If the issue is data-governance, product, or policy — route to the matching council or to a decision memo.

## Common failure modes

<div class="opi-margin-note" markdown="0">
  <span class="opi-margin-note__label">Catch these in the pre-brief</span>
  Most failure modes are caught earliest in the pre-brief between the Stat lead and the agency lead, not in the room. If two of these are present in the pre-brief, fix them before the session rather than during it.
</div>

- **Status creep** — name the performance problem at the top of every meeting; cut the standing tour of every metric.
- **Performative accountability** — read commitments aloud; close the loop in writing within three days; track to evidence of done.
- **Data theatre** — every chart answers a specific question; cut the rest.
- **Brand dilution** — hold the line on the five things; name other things working group, delivery review, product council.
- **Leader-shaped problems** — ask what kind of problem this is before deciding the intervention.

*Owned by the Deputy Chief Performance Officer. Reviewed quarterly. Update whenever the method, cadence, or QA bar changes.*
