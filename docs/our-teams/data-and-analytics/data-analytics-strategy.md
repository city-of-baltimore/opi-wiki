## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Foundations plus leverage**

Citywide Data & Analytics is OPI’s service for making Baltimore’s data accurate, accessible, governed, and useful. The Data & Analytics team builds and stewards the shared data, analytics, open data, governance, and AI-readiness infrastructure that enables performance management, service delivery, public transparency, and innovation at scale.

The team does not exist to become the default report shop for every agency. It exists to build foundations that make better reporting, analysis, dashboards, open data, and decision support possible across the City. The core posture is foundations plus leverage: build once, reuse many times.

The service is essential because every major city priority now depends on trusted data: CitiStat, 311, permitting, housing, public safety, cleaning, budget alignment, open data, digital products, and responsible AI. Without strong data stewardship, every other OPI service is weaker.

## SERVICE DEFINITION

**What this service is**

Citywide Data & Analytics strengthens Baltimore City’s data infrastructure, analytics capacity, open data program, and data governance practices across agencies. The goal is to make City data accurate, reliable, accessible, and useful for decision-making, transparency, and public accountability.

Activities include developing and maintaining data pipelines and shared datasets, producing analytics and data products, supporting data quality and governance standards, maintaining Open Baltimore, publishing high-quality open datasets, supporting data literacy, and preparing the City for responsible use of artificial intelligence and advanced analytics.

## WHY THIS WORK MATTERS

**A city cannot manage what it cannot trust**

A city cannot manage what it cannot trust. Every time an agency, Council office, resident, journalist, researcher, or leader asks a question, the quality of the answer depends on the quality of the data behind it. If the data is fragmented, undocumented, stale, or inconsistent, staff spend time rebuilding the foundation before they can solve the problem.

This is not sustainable. The City needs shared datasets, common definitions, documented lineage, open data standards, data quality routines, and reusable products that reduce duplication across departments. Data should not be a favor. It should be infrastructure.

> *This infrastructure is also the foundation for AI readiness. Responsible AI requires trusted sources, clear definitions, sensitive data controls, human oversight, and evaluation. AI without data stewardship is risk without value.*

## WHAT THE SERVICE OWNS

**Six lines of work**

**1. Data foundations.**

Citywide data platform, pipelines, shared datasets, APIs, lineage, refresh schedules, and reusable data models.

**2. Analytics and insight products.**

Dashboards, maps, analytic briefs, executive views, Stat data products, and decision-support tools.

**3. Data governance.**

Standards, definitions, stewardship models, access practices, quality rules, data sharing agreements, sensitivity classification, and governance councils.

**4. Open data and transparency.**

Open Baltimore stewardship, dataset inventory, publishing workflows, metadata, methodology cards, public feedback loops, and publication readiness.

**5. AI and advanced analytics readiness.**

Responsible AI framework, AI evaluation memos, human oversight standards, pilot use case review, model documentation, and decision-support analytics.

**6. Data literacy and communities of practice.**

Coaching, training, office hours, show-and-tells, and support for agency Data-Driven Officers and data stewards.

## WHAT THE SERVICE DOES NOT OWN

**Boundaries that protect the work**

Clear boundaries protect the team from becoming a bottleneck and protect agencies from losing ownership of their own work.

- It does not own performance accountability. Performance owns the routine; Data & Analytics provides trusted data and analysis.

- It does not own service redesign. The Innovation Lab leads service design, product discovery, and user research.

- It does not own cross-agency enforcement or implementation. Cross-Agency Delivery supports that work when activated.

- It does not replace BCIT. BCIT owns enterprise infrastructure, security, identity, networking, production environments, and enterprise technology operations.

- It does not own agency systems of record. Agencies own the systems that run their operations; Data & Analytics helps integrate and steward shared data when there is citywide value.

- It does not become the permanent analytics shop for every bespoke agency report. Ad hoc work should either become standardized, transition to an agency, or stop.

## OPERATING POSTURE

**Platform steward, not central fulfillment shop**

The Data & Analytics team should prioritize work that creates reusable value. A request is strongest when it supports CitiStat, mayoral priorities, public transparency, cross-agency delivery, open data, data governance, or a product that multiple agencies can use. The team can support ad hoc analysis when it unblocks a priority decision, but the long-term goal is to convert repeat requests into shared products.

> **Five intake questions: What decision will this support? Who will use it? Can it be reused? What data quality or governance risk exists? Who will sustain it after the first release?**

## HOW THE SERVICE PARTNERS ACROSS OPI

**Integration with other services**

### With Performance

The service provides trusted data, KPI definitions, dashboards, methodology cards, data quality notes, and analysis that supports performance routines. It does not run the Stat session or enforce follow-up.

### With the Innovation Lab

The service provides data, APIs, quality standards, and governance support for digital products, prototypes, AI pilots, and operational tools. It does not lead user research or service design.

### With Cross-Agency Delivery

The service provides shared data models, baseline metrics, and performance instrumentation that show whether delivery activations are changing conditions.

### With AdminOps

The service coordinates publication cadence, public-facing materials, Council briefings, and knowledge management for data products.

## OPEN DATA AS PUBLIC ACCOUNTABILITY INFRASTRUCTURE

**A product with users**

Open data should be treated as a product with users. Residents, journalists, researchers, Council, civic technologists, and community organizations need datasets that are current, documented, usable, and connected to the City’s priorities. The goal is not simply to post more files. The goal is to make public data easier to trust, understand, and use.

The service should maintain an inventory of datasets, prioritize publication based on public value and readiness, publish methodology cards for high-value datasets, and create feedback channels so users can report issues or request improvements.

## RESPONSIBLE AI READINESS

**A lightweight but real review process**

AI belongs in this service because AI builds on data foundations and governance. City use cases should be reviewed through a lightweight but real process: intake, risk tiering, privacy and security review, human-in-the-loop rules, logging, evaluation, and sunset criteria.

No production AI use case should proceed without an approved use case brief, defined data sources, documented assumptions, human oversight, and a plan to evaluate whether it works. The standard is not whether the technology is impressive. The standard is whether it improves a real decision or service while protecting residents and public trust.

## SUCCESS MEASURES

**How we know the foundation is strong**

- Share of priority datasets meeting documented quality and refresh standards.

- Number of Stat sessions, executive briefings, and public products backed by reusable data products rather than one-off extracts.

- Share of high-value open datasets that are current, documented, and assigned to a steward.

- Share of priority KPIs with methodology cards and data lineage.

- AI use cases reviewed with documented evaluation, human oversight, and sunset criteria.

- Agency participation in data governance, open data, and data literacy routines.

- Reduction in duplicate data cleaning and manual reporting for recurring priority questions.

## FY27 PRIORITIES

Create a KPI dictionary for priority performance measures used in CitiStat and public reporting.

Develop the first responsible AI review process and apply it to priority pilots.

**6. Reusable data products.**

Build reusable data products for 311, permitting, service delivery, public safety, housing, and other priority domains.

**7. Data literacy.**

Expand data literacy and communities of practice so agencies can use data responsibly without depending on OPI for every question.

## CLOSING

**A foundation, not a support function**

Citywide Data & Analytics is not a support function at the edge of OPI. It is one of the foundations that makes modern government possible. Trusted data enables performance management, service design, transparency, responsible AI, and better decisions. When the foundation is strong, every agency can move faster with more confidence. When it is weak, every improvement effort starts over.

> *The Data & Analytics team builds the foundation so Baltimore can run on evidence, share truth with the public, and improve services at scale.*
