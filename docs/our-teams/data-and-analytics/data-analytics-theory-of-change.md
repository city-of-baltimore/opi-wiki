## NORTH STAR

**Trustworthy data, reusable insight products, and a city that runs on evidence.**

*Strengthen Baltimore’s data infrastructure, analytics capacity, open data program, and data governance so that City data is accurate, reliable, accessible, and useful for decision-making, transparency, and public accountability. Mandate, governance, SLAs, and measurement detail follows.*

### 2. Operating scope (boundary lines)

*Full ownership / partnership / referral patterns are detailed in the About doc. This is the budget-facing summary.*

#### In scope for this cost center

- Data platform and pipelines — Citywide Data Platform, shared datasets, APIs, lineage, refresh standards.

- Analytics and insight products — executive dashboards, 311 data products, permit dashboards, GIS and spatial products, Stat dashboards.

- Open data — Open Baltimore program, dataset inventory, publishing discipline, methodology cards, public feedback channels.

- Data governance — definitions, ownership, quality SLAs, retention, equity cuts, data sharing agreements, Data Governance and Open Data Council support.

- Data literacy and AI/data readiness — agency coaching, communities of practice, evaluation memos for AI use cases, human oversight standards.

- Citywide data architecture coordination with BCIT and agency data teams.

#### Routed elsewhere

- Enterprise infrastructure / security / identity / production environments → BCIT.

- Agency operational systems of record → agencies.

- Service redesign and digital product builds → Innovation Lab.

- Performance routines themselves → Performance.

### 3. Engagement model

Work enters as a data product request, a Stat-driven KPI need, an open data publishing request, or a governance question. We use the standard OPI engagement model with a product-led delivery rhythm.

| **Stage**         | **What happens**                                                                            | **Outputs / decisions**                                                                          |
|-------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Intake            | Confirm sponsor, user, decision the data supports, urgency, and reuse potential.            | Intake stub; routing decision (data product, dataset publication, governance memo, or referral). |
| Problem statement | Clarify the decision context, the affected dataset(s), and the data quality starting point. | Two-page problem statement; assumptions and open questions logged.                               |
| Charter           | Define scope, datasets, KPIs, refresh cadence, owners, and definition of done.              | Data product charter; methodology card draft; dependency map with BCIT or agencies.              |
| Deliver           | Build pipeline, dashboard, dataset, or evaluation; QA before public release.                | Versioned data product; methodology card; user documentation; QA sign-off.                       |
| Sustain           | Establish refresh schedule, owner, monitoring, and sunset criteria.                         | Sustainment plan; runbook; data quality dashboard; annual review checkpoint.                     |

### 4. Theory of Change

| **Stage**          | **Concrete, measurable definition**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
|--------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Inputs             | Citywide Data Platform; agency data feeds with documented schemas; data engineering and analytics talent; Open Baltimore platform; data governance policies; KPI Dictionary; AI/data readiness framework; partnership agreements with BCIT and agency data teams.         |
| Activities         | Build and maintain pipelines and shared datasets; produce analytics and dashboards; publish high-quality open datasets; support data governance practices; coach agencies on data literacy; pilot and evaluate AI use cases; staff Data Governance and Open Data Council. |
| Outputs            | Documented, refreshed shared datasets and APIs; executive dashboards; 311, permit, and GIS data products; open datasets with methodology cards; KPI definitions linked to data lineage; AI evaluation memos; data quality dashboards.                                     |
| Outcomes (0–6 mo)  | Stat decks reference the same numbers across agencies; priority KPIs have documented owners and lineage; Open Baltimore publishing cadence improves; first reusable data products replace ad-hoc extracts.                                                                |
| Outcomes (6–18 mo) | Agencies build performance routines on reusable data products; cross-agency analyses become routine; AI pilots are evaluated and either scaled or sunset with evidence; data quality issues are surfaced and resolved through governance, not escalation.                 |
| Impact (2+ yr)     | Baltimore runs on trustworthy, well-governed data. Decisions are evidence-based by default. The public can verify what the City reports. Agencies see data as shared infrastructure, not a one-off favor.                                                                 |

### 7. Metrics, targets, and learning

- Data reliability — share of priority datasets meeting documented quality and refresh standards.

- Reuse — number of decisions and Stat sessions backed by reusable data products vs one-off extracts.

- Open data health — share of high-value datasets current; methodology card coverage; public feedback closed-loop rate.

- Decision support — share of executive briefings backed by KPI Dictionary entries.

- AI readiness — share of AI use cases with documented evaluation, human oversight, and sunset criteria.

- Agency capacity — Data-Driven Officers active in each priority agency; data literacy participation.

### 8. Operational handoffs across OPI services

*Partnership philosophy is in the About doc. These are the operational specifics.*

- To Performance — KPI definitions, dashboards, and data quality support for the Stat portfolio.

- To the Innovation Lab — datasets and APIs needed for service redesigns, digital products, and AI pilots.

- To Cross-Agency Delivery — shared data models for delivery activations; performance instrumentation for sustained improvements.

- To AdminOps — publishing cadence coordination for public-facing data products.

#### Appendix · Standard artifact suite

- Methodology card (definitions, sources, refresh cadence, caveats).

- Data product charter (decision supported, datasets, KPIs, owners, sustain plan).

- Data quality dashboard (priority datasets, refresh status, freshness, completeness).

- AI evaluation memo (use case, data, model, oversight, evaluation, sunset criteria).

- Data sharing agreement (parties, datasets, purpose, retention, security).

- Open data publication checklist (license, schema, methodology card, feedback path).
