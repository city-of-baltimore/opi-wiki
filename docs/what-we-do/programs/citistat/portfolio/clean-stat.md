### Public Purpose

Clean Stat focuses on Baltimore’s citywide cleanliness system. It coordinates sanitation, vacant property maintenance, illegal dumping response, enforcement, public realm conditions, community cleanups, and hotspot reduction.

### Staff Purpose

Staff use Clean Stat to coordinate work across agencies and partners. It should not duplicate Solid Waste Stat. Solid Waste Stat manages DPW sanitation performance; Clean Stat manages the cross-agency strategy required to make Baltimore visibly cleaner.

### Core Management Questions

- Which places are persistently dirty or experiencing repeat dumping?
- Are sanitation, enforcement, vacant property, and community cleanup strategies aligned?
- Which hotspots need cross-agency intervention rather than routine service?
- What evidence shows that conditions improved block by block?

### Starter Focus Areas

- Clean Block Scorecard and hotspot narrowing
- Illegal dumping prevention, response, and enforcement coordination
- Vacant lot maintenance and public realm condition improvements

### Standard Artifacts and Data Products

- Pre-meeting memo, Stat deck, follow-up memo, commitment tracker, and decision log.
- Core data product: Clean Block Scorecard and cleanliness hotspot dashboard.
- Public summary, annual report section, data quality notes, and SOP / process maps for major functions.
- Inspection checklist where field validation or service quality review is needed.

### Tiger Team / Delivery Triggers

- Persistent cleanliness hotspot despite routine service
- Illegal dumping pattern requiring enforcement, prevention, and cleanup coordination
- Neighborhood or corridor cleanliness issue requiring a tiger team
