DOT is one of the operating agencies moving to the [place-based model](../strategic-framework.md#the-place-based-model), alongside Housing. **Place is the primary unit of action:** each session takes one outcome, no more than two geographies, and two or three operational pressure points, rather than touring every transportation metric citywide.

### Public Purpose

DOT Stat works Baltimore's transportation outcomes where they are worst on the map: traffic safety on the high-injury network, plus the streets, parking, traffic calming, streetlights, resurfacing, and mobility infrastructure that shape a corridor's conditions. Citywide averages can improve while the most dangerous corridors stay stuck; place-based sessions concentrate attention where the need is greatest.

### Staff Purpose

Staff use DOT Stat to pick a priority corridor or intersection, diagnose why conditions are not moving using data and field intelligence, deploy resources against a small number of pressure points, and follow through until the place improves for residents, commuters, businesses, and neighborhoods.

### Core Management Questions

- For the priority outcome, which corridors or intersections carry the most concentrated harm or service gap?
- What do crash, 311, asset, and inspection data show about the chosen geography, and what caveats should the room understand?
- Which two or three pressure points, such as signal timing, striping, enforcement, or maintenance, would most change conditions there?
- What decision or cross-agency resource does the place need that only executive attention can unlock?

### Starter Focus Areas

- Traffic safety on the high-injury network: a priority corridor or intersection cluster
- Traffic calming and parking enforcement targeted to the priority geography
- Streetlight, pothole, and resurfacing performance where it affects the chosen place

### Standard Artifacts and Data Products

- Pre-meeting memo, Stat deck, follow-up memo, and follow-up tracker.
- Core data product: Transportation operations dashboard, with a corridor and intersection safety map to locate the priority geography.

### Tiger Team / Delivery Triggers

- Priority corridor needs sequencing across utilities, DPW, DGS, or public safety to change conditions
- Operational tool needed to improve enforcement, intake, or field workflow in the geography
- A place-based transportation intervention that requires sustained cross-agency delivery
