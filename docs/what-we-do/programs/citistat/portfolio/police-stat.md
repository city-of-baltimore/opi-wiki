Public safety is where the [place-based model](../strategic-framework.md#the-place-based-model) extends next, after Housing and DOT. Police Stat runs today as a Deputy Mayor-led agency Stat; as the model reaches public safety, sessions will increasingly focus on the specific places where a priority safety outcome is concentrated.

### Public Purpose

Police Stat focuses on public safety outcomes, crime trends, operational coordination, community safety priorities, and the systems that support effective policing. This Stat is Deputy Mayor-led, with OPI analytics and performance support.

### Staff Purpose

Staff use Police Stat to review violent crime trends, operational priorities, reporting and service issues, nuisance business coordination, multi-agency enforcement, and data quality.

### Core Management Questions

- What public safety outcomes are improving or worsening, and where?
- Which priority places, people, or issues require coordinated attention?
- Are follow-ups from reporting, enforcement, or multi-agency strategies being completed?
- Where is data quality limiting decision-making?

### Starter Focus Areas

- Violent crime and priority area trends
- Nuisance business and multi-agency coordination
- Telephone reporting, resident service, and follow-up quality

### Standard Artifacts and Data Products

- Pre-meeting memo, Stat deck, follow-up memo, and follow-up tracker.
- Core data product: Public safety performance dashboard.

### Tiger Team / Delivery Triggers

- Public safety issue requiring coordinated city resources beyond BPD
- Data quality or reporting issue affecting public safety decisions
- Place-based priority needing cross-agency delivery support
