# Baltimore 311 Explorer

{{ page_header(summary="A public explorer for Baltimore City 311 service requests.", category="OPI · PRODUCT") }}

The Baltimore 311 Explorer is a public tool for looking at the City's 311 service requests: the potholes, missed pickups, broken streetlights, and other issues residents report. It turns the 311 record into something anyone can browse, map, and summarize.

Explore it at [balt-311-explorer.fly.dev](https://balt-311-explorer.fly.dev).

## What you can do

The explorer shows 311 service requests on a map and in summary views, with filters so you can focus on a request type, an area, or a time range. It reads from a published 311 dataset that refreshes on a schedule, and it shows when the data was last updated so you know how current it is.

## How it fits OPI's work

The 311 Explorer is part of OPI's [Citywide Data and Analytics](../services/citywide-data-and-analytics.md) work: it takes an operational dataset the City already collects and makes it public and usable. The [Innovation Lab](../../about-us/our-teams/innovation-lab/index.md) builds it on the City's [data platform](baltimore-city-data-platform.md).
