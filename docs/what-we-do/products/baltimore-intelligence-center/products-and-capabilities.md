# Capabilities

{{ page_header(summary="The analytical and AI capabilities BIC can support through governed city data.") }}

## Product experiences

BIC can combine shared capabilities into focused experiences for approved city-service and performance workflows.

### City Services Intelligence

A situational view can combine trends, geography, and plain-language questions so an authorized user can explore city-service conditions from a citywide view to an appropriate local level. Answers remain grounded in governed data and shared definitions.

### StatGPT

A performance assistant can help authorized users find commitments, measures, and follow-ups in approved [CitiStat](../../programs/citistat/index.md) records. It is intended to make the performance record easier to search while preserving source references and access rules.

### ML decision support

Carefully evaluated analytical models can help teams compare scenarios and understand trade-offs for selected cross-agency problems. These uses require documented purpose, appropriate data, human review, and ongoing evaluation.

## The capabilities behind them

Every experience draws on the same governed capabilities, built once and reused. Shared definitions keep measures consistent. A relationship model connects relevant city entities without changing their source records. Analytical methods support evaluation and scenario comparison. Plain-language interfaces retrieve from approved sources and show where an answer came from. A shared design system keeps the experiences coherent and accessible.

## What makes them trustworthy

No product reaches raw data directly. Every AI-facing query passes through a governed tool that enforces the data tiers and refuses anything that has not cleared governance, and every answer is grounded in an approved definition and shows where it came from. The full model is in [Data Governance](../../programs/data-governance/index.md).
