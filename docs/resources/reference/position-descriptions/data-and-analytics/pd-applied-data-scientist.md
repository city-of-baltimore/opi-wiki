**■ Position Summary**

The Applied Data Scientist delivers applied analytics and data products that support priority city initiatives. The position combines analytics, applied research, and model development with product-oriented delivery. The role conducts ad-hoc analysis, produces reproducible research, develops features and models, and ships decision-ready products such as dashboards, reports, and lightweight web apps. The role also performs spatial analysis using GIS tools and collaborates with platform engineers to operationalize data pipelines and model serving.

This position reports to the Deputy Chief Data Officer and works closely with the engineering staff, the Analytics Lead, CitiStat analysts, and agency partners.

**■ Key Responsibilities**

**Analytics and Applied Research**

- Frame questions with stakeholders and convert them into analytic plans with clear measures and expected outcomes.

- Develop reproducible analyses using Python or R and SQL, including feature engineering, descriptive and inferential statistics, and predictive modeling where appropriate.

- Conduct policy and program analyses using administrative data from systems such as 311, permitting, water billing, and housing.

- Quantify impact and uncertainty; communicate assumptions and limitations in plain language.

**Data Products and Model Delivery**

- Build decision-ready products such as dashboards, parameterized reports, APIs, and lightweight web applications that surface analytic and model outputs.

- Package models for evaluation and operational use, including model versioning, scoring scripts, and monitoring plans.

- Collaborate with platform engineers to integrate products with the data platform and to establish refresh schedules and observability.

**GIS and Spatial Analytics**

- Perform geospatial joins, geocoding, and spatial analyses to support initiatives such as water-billing reconciliation and vacant-property work.

- Produce clear, accessible maps and spatial layers for internal use and public transparency where appropriate.

- Coordinate publishing of hosted feature layers to ArcGIS Online in collaboration with platform and GIS staff.

**Stakeholder Engagement and Documentation**

- Translate findings for non-technical audiences and prepare concise briefs and slides for executive and Council presentations.

- Partner with CitiStat and program staff to align metrics with service-delivery goals and to define actions and follow-through.

- Contribute to the open-data and transparency roadmap by identifying datasets and products suitable for publication.

- Maintain code, notebooks, and queries in shared repositories with versioning and clear README files.

- Create reusable templates and examples to accelerate future analyses and products; participate in code reviews.

**■ Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities**

**Technical and analytical**

- Proficiency in Python or R for analysis and modeling; strong SQL skills for dataset preparation.

- Experience building dashboards or web-based data products using tools such as Power BI, Tableau, R Shiny, Streamlit, or similar frameworks.

- Familiarity with GIS tools (ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, QGIS) and common spatial operations.

- Ability to implement data validation and monitoring for analytic products and model outputs.

**Communication**

- Clear communication skills for translating technical results to non-technical stakeholders.

- Ability to produce executive-ready briefs and slides.

- Ability to manage multiple priorities, maintain documentation, and deliver on a defined cadence.

**Operations management**

- Ability to adapt and apply administrative analysis techniques to specific projects.

- Ability to identify problem areas and recommend solutions based on logical alternatives.

- Ability to interpret and apply laws, rules, regulations, standards, and procedures.

- Ability to compile financial and operational data and to analyze charts, reports, and statistical and budgetary statements.

**■ Minimum Qualifications**

**Education**

- Bachelor’s degree in Data Science, Statistics, Economics, Computer Science, Public Policy, Urban Planning, Geography, or a related field; or equivalent experience.

**Experience**

- At least 3 years in applied analytics or data science, with experience producing dashboards, APIs, or web apps that surface analytic results; experience with GIS or spatial analytics.

**Equivalency**

An equivalent combination of education and experience will be considered.

**■ Working Conditions**

Employees in this class work a conventional work week. Work is performed in an office where work conditions are normal. Work requires minimal physical exertion. Hybrid telework is supported under OPI’s telework policy; staff are expected to be on site at City Hall a minimum of four days per week, with one day per week available as remote.
