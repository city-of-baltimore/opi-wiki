## Position Summary

The Executive Director and Chief Data Officer is the Mayor’s principal executive for performance, data, and innovation. The role sets OPI’s strategy, runs the citywide CitiStat program, leads Baltimore’s data and AI agenda, and partners with agencies to translate the Mayor’s priorities into measurable service improvements. The Executive Director represents OPI to the City Administrator, the Mayor, agency directors, the City Council, philanthropic partners, and the public.

The role is responsible for OPI’s four service portfolios (Director’s Office, Performance, Data and Analytics, and the Innovation Lab) and for the disciplined routines that turn analysis into commitments and commitments into delivery. The Executive Director also serves as the City’s Chief Data Officer with accountability for data governance, the Open Data Program, and the responsible adoption of analytics and AI in city government.

## Key Responsibilities

### Strategy and Executive Leadership

- Set OPI’s strategic direction in alignment with the Mayor’s priorities and the City Administrator’s operational agenda; publish and maintain the OPI strategic plan and annual goals.

- Build and steward executive relationships with agency directors, deputy mayors, the Bureau of the Budget and Management Research (BBMR), Baltimore City Information Technology (BCIT), and the City Council.

- Represent OPI to philanthropy, academic partners, peer cities, and national networks; develop partnerships and resources that extend OPI’s reach.

- Serve as the public face of OPI for testimony, briefings, and major announcements; ensure OPI communications are accurate, accessible, and aligned with the Mayor’s Office of Communications.

### Performance

- Convene and chair CitiStat sessions for the City Administrator and the Mayor; set the topic calendar and brief the Mayor on outcomes, decisions, and follow-through.

- Drive the citywide performance management system in partnership with BBMR, including agency performance plans, the Performance Tracker, and outcome-based budgeting alignment.

- Hold senior leaders accountable for closing the loop between analysis, decisions, and delivered service improvements.

### Data, Analytics, and AI

- Serve as the City’s Chief Data Officer; set citywide standards for data governance, data quality, lineage, and access.

- Direct the Open Data Program and citywide analytics products, including resident-facing dashboards, executive dashboards, and the Mayor’s reporting tools.

- Lead the responsible adoption of AI in city government, including evaluation standards, transparency requirements, and human-in-the-loop safeguards.

### Innovation Lab and Cross-Agency Delivery

- Direct the Innovation Lab’s service-design, civic-tech, and tiger-team work; ensure pilots are framed for adoption, evaluated honestly, and either scaled, sustained, or sunset.

- Activate cross-agency delivery routines (tiger teams) for the Mayor’s priority service problems; appoint delivery leads, hold checkpoints, and report progress.

### Office Leadership and Stewardship

- Build and lead OPI as an office that trusts each other and holds a high bar; recruit and develop the senior team; protect a culture of disciplined inquiry, plain-language writing, and follow-through.

- Steward OPI’s budget, grants, and personnel; partner with the Chief of Staff on operations, knowledge management, and SharePoint as the official record.

- Set OPI’s service standards, intake discipline, and quality bar; sponsor performance standards, the SOP library, and the office’s onboarding and offboarding programs.

## Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities

### Executive leadership

- Demonstrated success leading a performance, data, or innovation function in a complex public, civic, or mission-driven organization.

- Track record translating executive priorities into operating routines, measurable outcomes, and visible service improvements.

- Skill in convening, coaching, and holding senior leaders accountable in a visible setting that depends on trust and a high bar.

- Fluency in plain-language storytelling for executive, legislative, philanthropic, and public audiences.

### Performance, data, and AI

- Deep working knowledge of performance management frameworks, CitiStat-style routines, and outcome-based budgeting.

- Working understanding of modern data infrastructure (warehouses, pipelines, governance, open data) and responsible AI.

- Ability to set quality standards for analytics products and to read, challenge, and improve technical work without being the practitioner.

### Public administration and operations

- Knowledge of the principles, practices, and procedures of business and public administration; municipal budgeting and procurement; civil-service classification and personnel management.

- Ability to plan, organize, and direct the work of others across multiple disciplines and partner agencies.

- Ability to exercise judgment and discretion in applying laws, rules, regulations, and departmental policies.

- Ability to maintain confidentiality of sensitive information and to navigate political and operational sensitivities with care.

## Minimum Qualifications

### Education

- Master’s degree in Public Administration, Public Policy, Data Science, Business, Management, or a related field. Doctorate preferred.

### Experience

- 12+ years of progressively responsible leadership experience in performance management, data and analytics, public administration, or innovation, including at least 6 years leading senior teams in complex organizations.

### Equivalency

An equivalent combination of education and experience will be considered.

## Working Conditions

Employees in this class work a conventional work week. This position may be required to work evening and weekend hours and 24-hour callback in an emergency. Work is performed in an office where work conditions are normal. Work requires minimal physical exertion. Hybrid telework is supported under OPI’s telework policy; staff are expected to be on site at City Hall a minimum of four days per week, with one day per week available as remote.

## Supervision

**Supervision received.** Reports to the City Administrator with regular reporting responsibilities to the Mayor on citywide performance and the CitiStat program.

**Supervision given.** Provides executive oversight to OPI’s senior leadership team (the Chief of Staff, the Deputy Chief Performance Officer, the Deputy Chief Data Officer, and the Innovation Program Manager) and indirectly supervises the full OPI staff.
