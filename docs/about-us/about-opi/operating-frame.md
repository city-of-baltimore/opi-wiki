**FRONT MATTER**

**OPI works as one improvement cycle.**

OPI is the Mayor’s Office of Performance and Innovation. We help Baltimore City turn priorities into action, data into decisions, and commitments into measurable improvements residents and public servants can see and feel.

*We are a small, multidisciplinary office. Five services work together as one system. Each service has its own About document. This page explains how the system fits together.*

> *We do not improve services because someone reports on a problem. We improve them because the problem is defined clearly, the right people are in the room, the data is trusted, commitments are owned, and follow-up continues until conditions change.*

**The five services**

*Read each About \<Service\> document for the detail. Read these short statements together for how the cycle moves.*

**Performance and CitiStat**

Identifies where the system is falling short and creates routines for decisions and follow-through. Stewards CitiStat, agency performance planning, KPI standards, commitment tracking, and the executive performance rhythm.

**Citywide Data and Analytics**

Makes the problem knowable, trusted, measurable, and reusable. Builds the data platform, dashboards, open data, GIS, governance, and AI/data readiness so every other service runs on evidence.

**The Innovation Lab**

Designs, tests, and builds practical solutions with residents, staff, and agencies. Brings human-centered design, product discovery, civic technology, and responsible AI pilots to service problems.

**Cross-Agency Delivery**

Moves multi-agency commitments from agreement to implementation. A delivery overlay — not a standalone bureau — that activates when a priority outcome requires authorizers, sequenced commitments, and a sustainment owner.

**AdminOps**

Keeps the whole system prioritized, documented, resourced, communicated, and credible. The operating backbone for intake, portfolio visibility, briefings, communications, knowledge management, partnerships, and quality assurance.

**How the cycle moves**

| **Step**          | **What happens**                                                                                                                               | **Lead service**                       |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| See it            | A priority service is falling short. Stat surfaces the bottleneck. Data confirms the size, shape, and direction of the problem.                | Performance → Data and Analytics       |
| Understand it     | Research, journey mapping, and process mapping clarify how the service actually works for residents and staff.                                 | Innovation Lab                         |
| Decide and commit | Authorizers decide. Owners commit. The right operating routine is named — Stat, delivery activation, product build, or one-time decision memo. | Performance + Cross-Agency Delivery    |
| Build and ship    | Prototypes, MVPs, redesigns, SOPs, or system changes go into the field. Tools ship with owners and sustainment plans.                          | Innovation Lab + Cross-Agency Delivery |
| Sustain and tell  | Improvements are measured, sustained, and explained — to leadership, Council, partners, and the public.                                        | Performance + AdminOps                 |

**How to read the rest of the package**

- Each About \<Service\> doc explains the service in plain language: who we are, what we do, why it matters, what we own and do not own, how we partner, and what success looks like.

- Each service also has a paired Theory of Change doc with the operational backbone — mandate, engagement model, governance, SLAs, KPI metrics, handoff specifics, and the standard artifact suite.

- About answers “what is this service for, and how does it work in spirit?” Theory of Change answers “how is it staffed, governed, measured, and sustained?”

> *Together, these services help Baltimore City see clearly, act with discipline, and deliver improvements residents and public servants can see and feel.*
