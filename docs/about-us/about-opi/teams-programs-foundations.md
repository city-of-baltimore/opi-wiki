## Innovation Lab

Senior Product Engineer, and Product Engineer. Aspirational roles: Civic

> *From service problems to practical solutions.*

The Innovation Lab is OPI’s service improvement and applied innovation portfolio. It uses design, product, civic technology, AI pilots, and partnerships to help agencies design, test, and implement practical improvements.

### What Innovation Means at OPI

Innovation at OPI means practical improvement, not novelty. It is not "cool ideas," "special projects," or "technology for technology’s sake."

Innovation is how OPI helps Baltimore City understand service problems, design with the people closest to them, test practical solutions, and turn what works into better ways of operating.

### Owns

- Human-centered design and service design

- Product discovery and product management

- Resident and staff engagement

- Digital products for agency operations

- Operational workflow tools and data collection systems

- Responsible AI pilots focused on service improvement

- Strategic partnerships and civic innovation relationships

- Tiger Teams as an innovation method when discovery, design, or prototyping is required — the Lab provides the team and the Public Innovation Toolkit. The Lab is the capability (*how do we solve this?*); Cross-Agency Delivery is the service overlay (*how do we get multiple agencies to ship this together?*). When a Tiger Team’s work needs that overlay — named Authorizers at the Deputy Mayor or City Administrator level, decision rights across agencies, and sustainment commitments from each agency — it is chartered through Cross-Agency Delivery; otherwise through Innovation Lab intake under the agency director.

### Typical Outputs

- Product briefs and discovery memos

- Service maps and process maps

- Prototypes, pilots, and operational tools

- Resident and staff research findings

- Partnership concepts, grant ideas, and innovation briefs

### What Innovation Is Not

- A random ideas shop

- A shadow IT team

- A replacement for agency operations

- A place where unfunded special projects go

- A branding exercise

- A grant-chasing function disconnected from service delivery

- A team that builds tools without sustainment plans

### Where the Lab Sits

The Innovation Lab sits at the intersection of:

- Service design — understanding how residents and staff experience a service

- Product discovery — defining what tool, workflow, policy change, or process improvement would actually help

- Operational prototyping — testing small improvements before scaling them

- Civic technology — building or shaping practical digital tools where they help service delivery

- AI and applied innovation — testing emerging methods only when they solve a real operational problem

- Strategic partnerships — bringing in outside capacity when they help the City solve priority problems

## Function Boundaries and Handoffs

OPI’s portfolios are designed to overlap by intent, not by accident. The boundaries below describe how functions are divided when more than one portfolio could plausibly own a piece of work.

| **Function**                    | **Primary Lead**                                                 | **Boundary / Handoff Rule**                                                                                                                                                                                     |
|---------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Operational digital products    | Innovation Lab                                                   | Includes performance management portals, agency operations tools, data collection systems, parking enforcement products, intake forms, field tools, and applications that improve workflow or service delivery. |
| Insight and analytics products  | Data and Analytics                                               | Includes dashboards, analytic briefs, models, reusable datasets, open data releases, and tools whose primary purpose is to communicate insight or support analysis.                                             |
| Product discovery               | Innovation Lab                                                   | Innovation frames the problem, conducts user research, defines the service journey, identifies user needs, and develops product concepts and prototypes.                                                        |
| Technical implementation        | Innovation Lab or Data and Analytics                             | Operational service tools: Innovation owns product, coordinates implementation. Data/analytics tools: Data and Analytics owns implementation. Shared products require a joint product brief.                    |
| AI governance and AI innovation | Data and Analytics (governance); Innovation Lab (applied pilots) | Data and Analytics owns governance, responsible AI standards, model evaluation, and data readiness. Innovation owns pilots that improve workflows, experience, or operational service delivery.                 |
| Strategic partnerships          | Innovation Lab, with Director oversight                          | Partnerships should be tied to a portfolio priority, product roadmap, data capability, fellowship, grant, or innovation agenda. AdminOps brokers, tracks, briefs, communicates, and closes out commitments.     |
| Resident and staff engagement   | Innovation Lab                                                   | Innovation leads engagement that informs service design, product discovery, and problem framing.                                                                                                                |

## Foundations: How These Teams Work Together

OPI’s portfolios are designed to operate as one system. The full cycle reads:

> *CitiStat identifies performance problems. Data and Analytics helps explain them. The Innovation Lab designs and tests practical solutions. Cross-Agency Delivery helps coordinate implementation. AdminOps keeps the work visible, documented, and accountable.*

### Examples That Make It Real

- Redesigning 311 service request workflows

- Improving permitting processes and applicant communication

- Building field data collection tools for Downtown RISE or inspections

- Developing operational products for parking enforcement

- Testing AI-assisted resident communication

- Creating data collection systems for frontline teams

- Running Tiger Teams for priority service problems

- Mapping service journeys with residents and staff

- Designing tools that help agencies track, prioritize, and complete work
