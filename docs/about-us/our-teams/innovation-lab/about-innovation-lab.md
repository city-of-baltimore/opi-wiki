# About the Innovation Lab

{{ page_header(summary="Baltimore's in-house public-innovation team — how the city turns priority challenges into tested, adopted service improvements.") }}

The Innovation Lab is Baltimore's in-house public-innovation team. It works across agencies on the Mayor's priority challenges, using human-centered design, data, and disciplined delivery to turn service problems into solutions residents and staff actually use.

The Lab is an in-house innovation consultancy, not a department that runs services. It **facilitates and coaches** agency partners through a design-and-delivery process; it does not manage, and is not managed by, the departments it supports. It complements existing capacity, designs *with* residents and frontline staff, and hands a working improvement to the agency that will own it.

## What the Lab does

- **Design and research** — frontline listening, journey maps, service blueprints, root-cause analysis, and problem framing.
- **Product and engineering** — product discovery and management, and the design and build of **digital products**: resident-facing tools, operational workflow tools, and data-collection systems, built accessible and secure.
- **Applied AI pilots** — responsible AI use cases that improve a real decision or service, under the City's [responsible-AI review](../data-and-analytics/data-governance-framework.md).
- **Design and innovation sprints** — time-boxed engagements that bring agencies, residents, designers, engineers, and analysts together to diagnose, prototype, and pilot.
- **Reusable patterns** — a shared design system and a public innovation toolkit that future products build on.

## How the Lab works: the Path to Public Innovation

The Lab runs the four-phase method, adapting it to each challenge:

1. **Aim** — set an ambitious, measurable mission for a priority challenge, so a portfolio of work points at one resident outcome rather than scattered projects.
2. **Design** — understand and reframe the problem through design research with the residents and staff closest to it, then generate solutions through structured ideation and co-design.
3. **Activate** — build a portfolio of initiatives, prototype them cheaply, pilot the promising ones in real conditions, and scale what works.
4. **Sustain** — hand the improvement to the agency that owns it, with the documentation, training, and routines to keep it running after the Lab steps back.

Every engagement starts with the same five questions, and the Lab designs for handoff from day one:

> What problem are we solving? Who experiences it? What decision or workflow will change? What does success look like? Who will own the product after launch?

The Lab is honest enough to say when the right answer is not a product: sometimes it is a policy change, a staffing decision, or a better routine, and naming that is part of the work.

## The craft

The team is small and multidisciplinary: civic designers, an applied data scientist, a full-stack engineer, and an Innovation Program Manager. It works as one team across five skills: **civic design, data analysis, equity and engagement in design, delivery and change management, and storytelling.**

## What the Lab owns

- Human-centered and service design; product discovery and management.
- Resident and staff engagement and research.
- Prototypes, pilots, and in-house **digital products**, including operational and data-collection tools.
- Responsible-AI pilots focused on service improvement.
- The design system and the Lab's methods and toolkit.

## What the Lab does not own

- Agency operations and the long-term running of the tools it builds — handed off with a sustainment plan.
- Cross-agency coordination authority — that is [Cross-Agency Delivery](../../../what-we-do/services/cross-agency-delivery/index.md), the service OPI draws from across all its teams. When it charters a Tiger Team, the Lab provides method and people to it.
- Performance routines and CitiStat — the [Performance](../performance/index.md) team.
- Data governance, KPI definitions, and open data — [Data and Analytics](../data-and-analytics/index.md).
- Enterprise infrastructure, security, and production environments — BCIT.

## How the Lab partners across OPI

The Lab is OPI's hands-on build team, and it works *through* the other services rather than around them. **CitiStat** surfaces where a service is falling short. **Data and Analytics** provides the evidence, datasets, and platform its products run on. **Cross-Agency Delivery** coordinates when multiple agencies must move together. **AdminOps** carries intake, public narrative, and the publication channel that sustains the gain.

## What success looks like

- Priority service flows show higher task success and lower abandonment; rework, repeat contacts, and backlogs drop.
- The tools the Lab ships have clear owners, documentation, and sustained support after it steps back.
- AI pilots improve real operations responsibly, with evaluation and human oversight.
- A small portfolio of credible, visible wins ships each year.
