## PURPOSE

### What this service is for

The Innovation Lab turns service problems into tested, adopted solutions. It is OPI's service-improvement and applied-innovation capability — the craft of solving, used to make city services easier to access, track, and complete.

The Lab works one service at a time. It starts with how a service actually behaves for the people using it, designs a fix with the staff and residents closest to the problem, proves the fix in real conditions, and hands a working improvement to the agency that will run it.

<div class="opi-pullquote" markdown="0">A good Lab is honest enough to say when the right answer is not a product. Sometimes it is a policy change, a staffing decision, or a better routine — and naming that is part of the work.</div>

### Who we are

We are a small, multidisciplinary team: civic designers, an applied data scientist, a full-stack engineer, and an Innovation Program Manager.

We design *with* frontline staff and residents, not just for them. We map services end to end, test with the people who actually use them, and treat a prototype users cannot understand as not yet ready. Our disciplines are different; we work as one team.

### What we do

We run discovery, service design, user research, product management, prototyping, civic technology, applied AI pilots, and a build-to-handoff method: ship a minimum viable product (MVP) — the smallest version that proves the fix works — and hand it off cleanly to the agency that owns it.

Core work includes:

- Service discovery and design — frontline listening, journey maps, service blueprints, root-cause analysis, and problem framing.

- Product management and engineering — product briefs, prototypes, accessible and secure builds, QA, launch, and sustainment planning.

- Civic technology — operational workflow tools, data-collection systems, field tools, and public-facing tools where appropriate.

- Applied AI pilots — responsible AI use cases that improve a real decision or service, governed by the City's responsible AI review.

- Tiger Teams and design sprints — time-boxed engagements that bring agencies, residents, designers, engineers, and analysts together to diagnose, prototype, and pilot.

- Reusable patterns — a growing design system and the Public Innovation Toolkit that future products build on.

### Why it matters

Service problems usually look like technology, policy, or staffing problems in isolation. They are usually all three.

A resident may not know which agency owns a request. Staff may work across systems that do not talk to each other. A process may have grown through workarounds instead of intentional design. Without a team that can hold the whole problem and design across boundaries, agencies cannot move from analysis to action — and the resident keeps feeling the friction.

### How we work

We work time-boxed, evidence-led, and adoption-first.

We design for handoff from day one, run small, test before we scale, and reach for emerging methods like AI only when they solve a real operational problem. Every engagement starts with a real problem, named users, a sponsor, a decision, and a sustainment owner. We move from intake to discovery to design to prototype to pilot to sustainment, and we plan the handoff at the start, not the end.

### What we own

The team owns:

- Human-centered and service design.

- Product discovery and product management.

- Resident and staff engagement and research.

- Prototypes, pilots, and in-house digital products.

- Operational workflow tools and data-collection systems.

- Responsible AI pilots focused on service improvement.

- The Public Innovation Toolkit and the Lab's methods.

- The reusable design system and component library.

### What we do not own

The team does not own:

- Agency operations — the agency owns the workflow after handoff.

- Long-term operation of the tools we build — handed off with a sustainment plan.

- Cross-agency coordination authority — that belongs to [Cross-Agency Delivery](../../../what-we-do/services/cross-agency-delivery.md).

- Performance accountability or CitiStat routines — owned by Performance.

- Data governance, KPI definitions, or open data — owned by Data and Analytics.

- Enterprise infrastructure, security, and production environments — owned by BCIT.

*We partner closely on these areas. We do not replace the service owners.*

### How we partner across OPI

The Lab is OPI's hands-on build team, and it works through the other services rather than around them.

CitiStat surfaces the problem and tells us where a service is falling short. Data and Analytics provides the evidence, datasets, and platform our products run on. Cross-Agency Delivery coordinates when multiple agencies must move together. AdminOps carries intake, public narrative, Council readiness, and the publication channel that sustains the gain.

### What success looks like

Success means residents can access, track, and complete services more easily.

We know the work is succeeding when:

- Priority service flows show higher task success and lower abandonment.

- Rework, repeat contacts, and backlog drop in the services we touch.

- The tools we ship have clear owners, documentation, and sustained support after the Lab steps back.

- AI pilots improve real operations responsibly, with evaluation and human oversight.

- A small portfolio of credible, visible wins ships each year.

### Common outputs

- Service blueprints and journey maps.

- Discovery reports and options memos.

- Tested prototypes.

- Shipped MVPs and digital products.

- Operational workflow and data-collection tools.

- Product charters and runbooks.

- AI evaluation memos.

- Sustainment plans and handoff packages.

> *The Innovation Lab is not where special projects go to drift. It is how Baltimore designs, tests, and hands off service improvements that stick.*
