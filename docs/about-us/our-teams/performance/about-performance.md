# About Performance

{{ page_header(summary="How Baltimore turns priorities into measures, measures into decisions, and decisions into service improvements residents can feel.", category="ABOUT · SERVICE") }}

The Performance team runs Baltimore's citywide performance management: the routines that help leaders and agencies set clear priorities, measure what matters, review progress with discipline, and follow through until services improve. Its flagship routine is [CitiStat](../../../what-we-do/programs/citistat/index.md), the executive performance program the team operates day to day.

The team owns the method, not the agencies' operations. Agencies deliver services; Performance brings the structure, data, facilitation, and follow-through that turn a performance problem into a decision and a decision into a result.

## What the team does

- Operate the CitiStat program — sessions, pre-memos, follow-up memos, and follow-up tracking — for the citywide portfolio of Stats.
- Support agency performance planning: goals, KPI trees, owners, and cadences aligned to the Mayor's Action Plan.
- Maintain, improve, and update as appropriate the City's performance measures, KPI definitions, and performance plans.
- Implement performance routines that support the Mayor's Action Plan.
- Maintain and update the City's publicly facing performance products.
- Track follow-ups to closure with evidence, and route issues that need executive decisions, delivery, data support, or service redesign.

## How the work fits together

Performance management only matters if it changes decisions. Three jobs stay distinct and reinforce each other:

- The **Mayor's Action Plan** sets citywide priority outcomes.
- **Agency Performance Plans** translate those into each agency's annual commitments and measures.
- **CitiStat** finds where a required result is not moving, concentrates attention on a few problems, and drives the tactics to move them.

If a problem is not important enough to sit in the Mayor's Action Plan or an Agency Performance Plan, it is not important enough for CitiStat. Every Stat holds to the [four tenets](../../../what-we-do/programs/citistat/method-playbook.md#the-four-tenets-of-stat): timely, accurate information shared by all; rapid deployment of resources; effective tactics and strategies; and relentless follow-up.

### The lightest routine that works

Not every performance question needs a standing Stat. Some belong in a one-time decision memo, a single delivery review, or a focused data sprint. The team's job is to route each issue to the lightest routine that will actually change the outcome, and to reserve CitiStat for problems that need recurring leadership attention, reliable-enough data, and follow-up over multiple cycles.

## The quality bar

A Stat only earns leadership's time if the numbers hold up. Every Stat product passes an independent review by the **CitiStat Program Manager** before it is presented. Data sources are checked for reliability and audited for how the data is actually collected and entered. Every figure names its source and pull date, code is documented so the analysis is reproducible, and visuals meet the City's standards for clarity and accessibility.

## What success looks like

Success is a performance system that is useful, trusted, and acted on outside the meeting:

- **Routine reliability** — sessions happen as scheduled; pre-memos and follow-up memos are on time; follow-ups are tracked and closed with evidence.
- **Better decisions** — sessions produce decisions, follow-ups, or measurable learning, not status recaps.
- **Data integrity** — priority measures carry an owner, formula, source, cadence, and caveats.
- **Service improvement** — priority services show measurable gains residents can feel: shorter cycle times, smaller backlogs, fewer repeat problems.
- **Trust** — leadership relies on the numbers, and residents and Council can see what changed.
