# Silence Teams Notifications While Sharing Your Screen

{{ page_header(summary="Short how-to for preventing notification popups during screen sharing in Microsoft Teams.") }}

## Before you start

Microsoft Teams can automatically switch your status to `Do not disturb` while
you are sharing your screen. If you prefer, you can also set that status
manually before a meeting starts.

## Option 1: Turn on automatic Do Not Disturb

1. Open Microsoft Teams.

2. Select your profile picture.

3. Open `Settings`.

4. Go to `Privacy`.

5. Turn on the setting that sets your status to `Do not disturb` while sharing
   your screen.

## Option 2: Set Do Not Disturb manually

1. Select your profile picture.

2. Open your current status menu.

3. Choose `Do not disturb`.

4. If needed, set a duration before the meeting begins.

## Optional cleanup

- Add priority contacts in `Settings -> Privacy` if certain people still need to
  reach you.

- If you also want to silence non-Teams notifications, use your operating
  system's focus or notification settings.

## What success looks like

When `Do not disturb` is active, Teams shows the status next to your profile and
suppresses popup interruptions while you present.
