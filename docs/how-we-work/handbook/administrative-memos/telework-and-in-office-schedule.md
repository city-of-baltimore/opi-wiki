Beginning Monday, February 23, 2026, OPI moves from its current policy of four days per week in office to a rotating weekly schedule that alternates between four days and three days onsite. In the first week, staff are onsite four days: Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are the core days, and you set the fourth day with your manager. Each following week alternates between four days and three days onsite, holding the core days constant. On three-day weeks, Monday and Friday are the remote days.

Example:

| Week of      | Schedule                                                                |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| February 23  | All staff onsite 4x (1 remote day, set with your manager)               |
| March 2      | All staff onsite 3x (Monday and Friday as remote days)                  |
| March 9      | All staff onsite 4x (1 remote day, set with your manager)               |

This adjustment supports collaboration, culture, and delivery as our team takes
on more ambitious goals in FY26. This administrative guidance is aligned with
the City of Baltimore's hybrid work framework and does not supersede any rights
or obligations under City HR policy, ADA, FMLA, or other applicable laws.
Requests for accommodation will continue to be handled through the city's
established HR processes.

Core days keep our work aligned and move decisions faster. In-person time
supports the impromptu conversations, brainstorming, and shared wins that build
OPI's culture, and a consistent rhythm gives everyone the same predictability
about when teammates are available.

## Core Expectations

These expectations set OPI's in-office rhythm and its remote-day flexibility.

### In-Office Days

- **Core Days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday are mandatory in-office days for everyone.

- **Fourth Day:** Work with your manager to select either Monday or Friday as your additional in-office day.

- **Flexibility:** Certain projects or in-person meetings may require attendance on a specific Monday or Friday. In those cases, work with your manager to swap your fourth day as needed. Swaps do not roll over to future weeks.

### Remote Days

- Each staff member has one remote day per week.

- Remote days do not bank or roll over.

- Regular weekly WFH days should be posted on the OPI team calendar. Any exceptions or swaps should also be reflected on the calendar so that expectations are clear across the team.

### Core Hours

- All staff are expected to work the typical 7.34-hour work day.

- For in-office days, everyone is expected to be present in the office between 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.

- Staff may arrive earlier or leave later depending on individual schedules, but all team members must be on-site during core hours.

### Full-Day Presence

- In-office days are expected to be full workdays. Split or partial in-office days are not permitted.

- If an unavoidable commitment arises, staff should work with their manager and follow City leave or flex-time policies. Managers are expected to apply exceptions consistently and equitably across staff.

## FAQs

**Q: What if I need to be remote on one of the required core days (Tue-Thu)?**  
**A:** Work with your manager to request an exception in advance. Exceptions are
limited, must be approved, and will be applied equitably.

**Q: Can I swap my remote day for a different week or pool them?**  
**A:** No. Remote days do not bank or roll over.

**Q: What if I have a recurring family or personal obligation?**  
**A:** Speak with your manager. Case-by-case accommodations will be evaluated in
line with City HR policies.

**Q: What if I'm sick or have an emergency?**  
**A:** Standard leave and telework policies apply. Communicate promptly with
your manager.

**Q: Will there be check-ins on compliance?**  
**A:** Yes. Managers will monitor attendance and core hours to ensure
consistency across the team. Repeated violations of attendance will be subject
to HR/corrective actions.
