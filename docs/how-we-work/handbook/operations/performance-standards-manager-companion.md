## How to use this companion

This is a manager-only companion to the OPI Performance Standards (Staff) guide. It defines the calibration packet, the calibration questions, and a short bias check. Use it before every quarterly check-in, mid-cycle 360-lite, and end-of-cycle calibration, and bring it to calibration meetings.

Keep the point of calibration in view. It is there for fairness and shared standards. It is not stack ranking, it does not reward politics, and it does not punish people for constraints outside their control when they raised the risk in time.

## The calibration packet

Each manager brings a calibration packet for each staff member. The packet is the basis for calibration. Without it, calibration is opinion, not evidence.

| **\#** | **Item**                      | **What it contains**                                                                                                                               |
|--------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1      | Role Charter (start of cycle) | Top priorities (3–5), success measures, role craft dimensions (2–4), definition of done, required artifacts and where they live, check-in cadence. |
| 2      | Self-review (1 page)          | Staff narrative against the Appendix B prompts: outcomes, quality moment, miss and learning, system improvement, growth plan.                      |
| 3      | Manager review (1–2 pages)    | Overall rating proposal with rationale, impact narrative, core and craft ratings with behavior anchors, evidence links, growth plan.               |
| 4      | Evidence bundle (5+ links)    | Final artifacts and supporting materials. At least one closed-loop example and one QA discipline example.                                          |
| 5      | 360-lite notes (optional)     | Two to four short quotes from peers or partners, anonymized if needed. Focused on impact and collaboration.                                        |

## Calibration questions

Ask these five questions for each staff member during calibration, and anchor every claim to a link in the evidence bundle.

1.  What changed because of this person’s work, and what is the evidence?

2.  What was the scope, and what constraints shaped the cycle?

3.  What did they own, and what did they support?

4.  How was risk handled across accuracy, approvals, privacy, and QA?

5.  Did they build assets others can reuse, such as templates, SOPs, datasets, components, or runbooks?

## Bias check

Run this check before finalizing a rating. If you answer yes to any item, return to the evidence bundle and re-anchor your rating.

- Am I overweighting the last month?

- Am I scoring visibility over substance?

- Did I use personality labels instead of behaviors?

- Did I apply different standards based on communication style?

## Calibration principles

Calibration exists for fairness and shared standards, and a few principles keep it honest. It does not enforce a distribution, so it is not stack ranking. It does not reward politics: visibility is not impact, and being seen is not the same as performing. It does not penalize escalation, so staff who flagged risk early and worked the problem are not marked down when constraints outside their control blocked the outcome. And it holds across teams: two staff members with comparable scope, evidence, and outcomes should land at comparable ratings, whatever team they sit on.

## Manager checklist before calibration

- Role Charter is finalized and dated.

- Self-review is received and read.

- Manager review is drafted, with overall rating proposal and rationale.

- Evidence bundle has at least five links, including one closed-loop example and one QA discipline example.

- 360-lite input has been collected (if applicable) and summarized.

- Bias check has been completed.

- Disagreement pathway has been explained to the staff member if a difficult rating is proposed.

## Difficult conversations

### When proposing a 2

- Bring specific evidence and a draft 30-day reset plan to the conversation.

- Frame the conversation around expectations and supports, not personality.

- Document the conversation, the plan, and the next check-in date.

### When proposing a 1 or after a trust breach

- Coordinate with the Director’s Office and HR before the conversation.

- Narrow scope to safe tasks while confidence is restored.

- Document everything in writing and follow City HR procedures.

### When a staff member disagrees

- Re-walk the evidence bundle together. Listen first.

- If unresolved, route to the second-level reviewer per the disagreement pathway.

- Document the rationale for the final rating during calibration.

## Owner and cadence

Owned by the OPI Director’s Office. Reviewed annually alongside the Staff v2 guide. Updates that change rating definitions, weights, or process are versioned and announced before the next cycle begins.
