**■ Why onboarding matters**

Great onboarding is a retention and performance lever. Staff who land well stay longer, contribute earlier, and trust the office more. Staff who land poorly cost the team energy in ways that compound for months. Treat onboarding as critical infrastructure.

> *The first 90 days are the easiest time to set high standards. After that, expectations harden.*

**■ Your minimum standard**

Every OPI manager owns the following from offer-accept through day 100. None of these are optional.

| **When**  | **Task**                                       | **How**                                                                                                   |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Pre-start | Confirm scope and success.                     | Translate the job description into three to five concrete outcomes the new hire will be measured against. |
| Pre-start | Day-one readiness: accounts, access, hardware. | Coordinate with AdminOps and IT four to six weeks ahead. Day-one readiness is non-negotiable.             |
| Pre-start | Assign an onboarding buddy.                    | Pick a peer who is generous with time and good at navigating culture.                                     |
| Pre-start | Build a first-week schedule.                   | Welcome meeting, OPI overview, BCIT, HR, two portfolio overviews, and shadow at least one Stat session.   |
| Week 1    | Set explicit expectations.                     | Communication norms, work quality, escalation paths, documentation expectations.                          |
| Week 1–2  | Introductions to key partners.                 | Internal partners and agency contacts. Explain decision rights so the new hire is not surprised.          |
| Day 10    | Finalize the 30/60/90 plan.                    | In writing. Use the OPI 30/60/90 template. Confirm stakeholders and deliverables.                         |
| Day 30    | Run a structured 30-day check-in.              | Use OPI performance dimensions as the rubric. Capture agreements in writing.                              |
| Day 60    | Run a structured 60-day check-in.              | Calibrate ownership expansion, quality and reliability.                                                   |
| Day 90    | Conduct a 90-day review.                       | Confirm ownership, expectations, and growth plan. Set next-quarter objectives.                            |
| Day 100   | Day-100 readout with leadership.               | 15–30 minutes. Use the readout template.                                                                  |

**■ The four conversations**

Schedule these four conversations explicitly during the first 30 days. They are easy to skip and expensive to skip.

**1. The role and outcomes conversation**

Translate the job description into three to five outcomes the new hire is responsible for. Be specific. Include what success looks like at day 30, day 60, and day 90.

**2. The norms conversation**

How we communicate, how we make decisions, when to escalate, and what quality looks like. Walk through the OPI Quick Reference Handbook together.

**3. The relationships conversation**

Who matters in this role. Internal: portfolio peers, the Chief of Staff, the Executive Director. External: agency contacts and partners. Explain decision rights and sensitivities.

**4. The growth conversation**

What the new hire wants to learn. What skills and exposure they need. How you will create opportunities for them.

**■ How to assign the first early win**

A good early win is small, real, and shippable in the first three to four weeks. It builds confidence and gives the new hire concrete experience with our tools and norms.

- Pick something that reduces friction, improves clarity, or closes a known gap.

- Define done before they start. What does "complete" look like?

- Give them a real partner who can review and sign off.

- Protect time for it. Do not let ad hoc churn crowd it out.

- Celebrate it when it ships. Use a Teams channel or all-staff moment.

**■ Coaching on OPI performance dimensions**

Use OPI’s five performance dimensions as your check-in rubric. Be specific. Tie feedback to concrete examples.

| **Dimension** | **What to look for**                                                                             |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Reliability   | Are commitments tracked? Met on time and at quality? When something slips, is it surfaced early? |
| Quality       | Is the work accurate, well-documented, useful to others, and reproducible?                       |
| Judgment      | Does the new hire frame problems clearly, weigh tradeoffs, and escalate well?                    |
| Collaboration | Do they work well with peers, partners, and agencies? Do they share credit?                      |
| Growth        | Are they building skills, giving and receiving feedback, and improving over time?                |

**■ Common mistakes to avoid**

- **Treating day-one readiness as someone else’s problem:** It is yours, not IT’s. Coordinate access and hardware four to six weeks ahead.

- **Overloading the calendar in week one:** Leave space for unstructured time, reading, and one-on-ones.

- **Skipping the onboarding buddy:** Buddies are about culture and navigation, not redundant management.

- **Vague expectations:** "Do good work" is not a target. Use outcomes and OPI performance dimensions.

- **Letting ad hoc churn crowd out the early win:** Use WIP limits. Say "not now." Protect the early win.

- **Waiting until day 90 to give feedback:** Calibrate weekly. Surface issues early in writing.

**■ Templates and prompts**

**30-day check-in script**

Open with: "What is going well?" Then ask:

- What was clearer than you expected? What was less clear?

- Where do you feel you are calibrating? Where are you confident?

- What do you need from me that I am not yet providing?

- Are there blockers we should escalate?

Close with one specific change for the next 30 days. Document it.

**60-day check-in script**

- Walk me through what you have shipped. What was hardest?

- What is your slice of work now? Is the scope right?

- Calibrate against OPI performance dimensions. Where are you strong? Where do you want to grow?

- What relationships do you still need to build?

Close with two outcomes for the next 30 days. Document them.

**90-day review script**

- Review concrete outcomes against the 30/60/90 plan.

- Calibrate against OPI performance dimensions with specific examples.

- Confirm ownership for the next quarter.

- Set growth objectives.

Close with the day-100 readout plan and the leadership audience.

**■ Manager commitments to share with new hires**

> *Copy these into the new hire’s welcome email or share them in the first one-on-one.*

- I will ensure day-one readiness: accounts, access, workspace, and a clear first-week plan.

- I will provide weekly one-on-ones and remove blockers quickly, or escalate within 24 hours.

- I will protect focus and set clear priorities, including WIP limits and "not now" calls.

- I will give candid feedback early and tie it to OPI performance dimensions.

- I will create opportunities for early wins and visible, meaningful ownership by day 90.

**■ Appendix. Manager onboarding readiness checklist**

Run through this checklist two weeks before the new hire’s start date. Check each item or escalate the gap.

- Workday entry confirmed (start date, location, telework schedule, supervisor, cost center).

- BCIT ticket open for laptop and peripherals. Ticket number recorded.

- Required software and licenses identified and requested.

- SharePoint sites, Teams channels, and Planner or Jira project access provisioned.

- Onboarding buddy assigned and notified.

- Welcome email sent with day-one logistics.

- Day-one schedule built and scheduled.

- Manager one-on-one cadence scheduled (weekly).

- Daily 10–15 minute check-ins scheduled for the first week.

- First-month onboarding meeting set scheduled (OPI overview, CitiStat, AdminOps, Data, Innovation Lab, BCIT, HR).
