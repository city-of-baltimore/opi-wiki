Essential and Emergency Essential Designations for Continuity of Operations

**Purpose**

To support continuity of operations during emergencies and significant citywide
events, this memo establishes OPI staff designations as Essential or Emergency
Essential effective Monday, March 9. These designations clarify reporting
expectations when city operations are disrupted (for example: severe weather,
public safety incidents, critical systems outages, or other emergency
activations).

This memo is intended to align with citywide emergency protocols and applicable
HR guidance. Specific pay, leave, and reporting rules remain governed by city HR
policy, bargaining unit agreements, and any event-specific direction issued by
the Department of Human Resources (DHR) or the Office of the Labor Commissioner
(OLC).

**Staff designations (effective March 9, 2026)**

Designations support continuity of operations during City activations (for
example: severe weather, public safety incidents, critical systems outages, or
other emergency declarations).

| **Designation** | **Who** | **Reporting expectation** |
|---|---|---|
| Essential | OPI leadership and Principal Staff (Operations Officer V and above). | Required to report to duty or perform remote duties as directed during emergency operations or continuity activations, unless explicitly released. |
| Emergency Essential | All other OPI staff. | May be required to report to duty or perform remote duties as directed based on the incident and operational needs. Be prepared for activation and schedule changes. |
| Non-Essential | No OPI positions are designated Non-Essential at this time. | Not applicable for OPI at this. |

**How activation works**

- The OPI Executive Director or Chief of Staff will issue an OPI-specific
  posture for staff, including: reporting instructions, priority work, coverage
  schedule, and communications cadence.

- Emergency Essential activation is event-based. Not all events require full
  activation. Leadership will determine which teams and functions are needed.

- If the city issues a closure, delayed start, telework posture, or emergency
  activation, OPI staff may be required to report to work.

**Reporting expectations**

**Essential** (OPI leadership and principal staff — Operations Officer V and above)

- Maintain availability for incident coordination and executive decision support during activation windows.

- Provide coverage plans for your areas, including delegation and escalation paths.

- Ensure continuity for critical OPI responsibilities (including executive support, critical systems/data, cross-agency coordination, public-facing communications).

**Emergency Essential** (all other OPI staff)

- Monitor city and OPI communications and be reachable during activation windows.

- Be prepared to support time-sensitive operational needs (including analysis, communications support, data products, coordination, issue resolution).

- Follow supervisor instructions on reporting location, telework posture, hours, and tasking.

**Non-Essential** (for reference)

- No OPI positions are designated Non-Essential at this time.

**Communication channels**

During activations, official updates and instructions will be communicated
through the channels below. Staff should prioritize the most recent official
guidance.

- Microsoft Teams (OPI channels and direct supervisor communications)

- Email (as needed for formal directives)

- Phone/SMS call tree for urgent escalations or time-critical notices

**Supervisor responsibilities**

- Confirm team contact lists and escalation coverage (including alternates) at
  least quarterly.

- Ensure staff understand role-based expectations and activation procedures.

- During activations, provide clear tasking, priority sequencing, and expected
  availability windows.

**What you should do now**

- Ensure your phone number and emergency contact details are current per city HR
  processes and internal OPI records.

- Confirm with your supervisor how you will receive urgent activation notices
  (Teams vs phone/SMS).

- Review the OPI activation checklist when published (location will be
  communicated via Teams and posted to SharePoint).

**Questions**

If you have questions about your designation or reporting expectations, contact
your direct supervisor first, or Rakeim Young, Chief of Staff, for
clarification.
