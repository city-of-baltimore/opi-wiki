# Storyteller / Brand Review — OPI Foundations

**Persona:** Communications & narrative strategist. Test: one crisp story, one strong metaphor, zero jargon drift.

## Scenario findings

**1. OPI in one sentence (site only):** "OPI is the Mayor's Office that runs Baltimore's performance and innovation work in public — using a signal-to-solution loop where CitiStat spots the problem, Data explains it, the Innovation Lab designs and tests the fix, Cross-Agency Delivery coordinates it across agencies, and AdminOps sustains the gain." That sentence is fully assemblable from `index.md` line 21 — good. The metaphor exists and is repeatable.

**2. Central metaphor — the signal-to-solution loop.** It is strong enough to be the front door, and it nearly is: home, `how-we-work/index.md`, and `how-work-moves-through-opi.md` all lead with it. The problem is it is told in at least **three competing wordings**: home uses an arrow chain ("identifies the problem → explains it →"); `how-we-work/index.md` uses a numbered list ("CitiStat identifies the problem. Data explains it."); `how-work-moves-through-opi.md` uses both a bolded blockquote AND a 5-column table. Add `teams-programs-foundations.md` (line 7 and again line 15 — the **same sentence twice on one page**) and `mission-vision-identity` paraphrases, and you have ~4 phrasings of one idea. A loop should sound like a chant. This one is recognizable but not yet memorized-on-first-read.

**Naming nit inside the metaphor:** it is called a "loop" but every telling is drawn as a straight 5-step **line** (1→2→3→4→5). Nothing closes back to step 1. Either show CitiStat re-checking the sustained gain (true loop) or rename it the "signal-to-solution **path/chain**." Right now the word and the picture disagree.

**3. Jargon & acronym audit — PASS on the headline.** "CAD" never appears as Cross-Agency Delivery anywhere. The only "CAD" hits are on two Baltimore Intelligence Center product pages referring to Computer-Aided Dispatch (emergency dispatch) — which is exactly the collision `glossary.md` line 55 warns about, so those uses are correct and even validate the ban. "Cross-Agency Delivery" reads cleanly and is consistently spelled in full; the approved short form "x-agency delivery" is defined once and used sparingly. *Other jargon risks:* "Authorizer / Senior Responsible Owner (SRO) / commitment ledger / Tiger Team / Stat pre-memo" stack up fast in `how-work-moves-through-opi`. SRO appears as an acronym in the handoff table (line 98) but is spelled out elsewhere — define-on-first-use drift. "Stat" vs "CitiStat" vs "Citywide Performance Management" are three names for adjacent things and a partner will conflate them.

**4. Same idea, two voices (drift/redundancy).** Confirmed in three places: (a) the loop, above; (b) `teams-programs-foundations.md` repeats its loop blockquote verbatim twice in 8 lines — pure duplication; (c) theory-of-change is handled *well* — `theory-of-change-summaries.md` explicitly says it "carries no independent summaries… one place — and only one place — to maintain each service's logic." That is the model the loop should copy: one canonical telling, everything else links to it.

**5. IA verdict — "Our Work" parent over Teams + Programs + Products.** **Muddies it.** Today the nav distinguishes Teams (who/budget), Programs (CitiStat), Products (tools), and the *story* lives in How We Work. Collapsing three different object-types under "Our Work" hides the loop and blurs the home-page taxonomy table (`index.md` line 27) that carefully separates Teams/Services/Programs/Products. If anything, the front-door verb-noun is **"How We Work"** — that is where the metaphor lives. Keep Teams/Programs/Products as nouns; do not fuse them. Verdict: do not adopt.

## FORCED OUTPUTS

1. **One-line reaction:** *Agree-with-changes* — narratively close, but loop drift and a duplicated blockquote keep it from production-ready.
2. **Top 3 blockers:** (a) Loop told in 3–4 wordings across home / how-we-work / how-work-moves — pick ONE canonical sentence; (b) `teams-programs-foundations.md` prints the loop twice on one page; (c) "loop" is named but drawn as a line — metaphor/visual mismatch.
3. **Top 3 low-risk high-impact fixes:** (a) Define one canonical loop sentence, quote it everywhere else verbatim (mirror the theory-of-change pattern); (b) delete the duplicate blockquote in `teams-programs-foundations.md`; (c) spell out SRO on first use in `how-work-moves-through-opi.md` line 98.
4. **Naming issue:** "loop" vs the linear 1→5 rendering — close it back to CitiStat or rename it.
5. **Drift risk:** each team page will quietly re-paraphrase the loop in its own voice; without a single canonical string, the five wordings become ten.
6. **Audience need missed:** a resident / peer-city reader has no 30-second "what is OPI and why should I care" landing — the hero is internally framed ("how a modern office runs"); add a plain public one-liner.
7. **Page that should be canonical (treated as such):** `how-work-moves-through-opi.md` is the canonical loop narrative but home and `how-we-work/index.md` both re-tell rather than quote it — make those excerpt-and-link, not re-author.
