# Round 2 Review — CitiStat Analyst (Staff)

Daily-use perspective. I onboard hires, prep Stats, and hunt operating refs.

## Scenario run

**1. CitiStat = Program.** Confirmed. `/what-we-do/programs/citistat/` opens with "a program supported by all OPI teams," names the Performance team as its operational home, and frames the signal→solution loop cleanly. It does NOT read as a team. This is the single biggest win of the reorg — the old "is CitiStat a team?" confusion is gone.

**2. My team's ToC in 1–2 clicks.** Mostly yes, but mislabeled. Our Teams > Performance > "Performance Management Theory of Change." Two clicks. Problem: it's badged "SERVICE THEORY OF CHANGE · 01 OF 05" and titled *Performance Management*, not *Performance*. So the team's ToC is actually the *service* ToC wearing the team's hat. **Roster:** found in two clicks at How We Work > Organization > Team and Roles — "Team Roster" + "Roster by Portfolio," grouped by portfolio and cost center. Merge worked; this is genuinely better than the old split.

**3. Stat pre-memo from the Method Playbook alone.** Yes — no forcing back to the Strategic Framework. The Playbook carries the 12-step analyst cycle, the T-2/48-hour send-out rule, pre-memo naming convention, the Inspector field-validation tie-in, and points to Templates + Quality Standards for the artifact and the QA checklist. I can prep a pre-memo end-to-end without leaving the staff-facing docs. The "Read alongside Strategic Framework" link is a pointer, not a dependency. Clean.

**4. Handbook / Organization grouping.** Helps. Routines (Onboarding, Admin Memos, How-To, Operations) sit together under How We Work > Handbook and the landing page is a real index, not a stub. One friction: the nav visually nests Handbook *near* Organization, but the URLs are siblings (`/how-we-work/handbook/` vs `/how-we-work/organization/`). Minor, but a hire told "it's under Organization" will fumble.

**5. Performance/CPM/CitiStat language.** This is the soft spot. Three near-twins live one nav-level apart: the **Performance** team, the **Citywide Performance Management** service, and the **CitiStat** program. The team ToC borrowing the service title ("Performance Management Theory of Change") collapses two of them into one breadcrumb. A new analyst cannot tell from labels alone which noun owns what.

---

## Forced outputs

1. **Reaction:** agree-with-changes. The bones are right; the naming layer needs one cleanup pass before it's production-ready.
2. **Top 3 blockers:**
   - Team "Performance" vs service "Performance Management" vs program "CitiStat" are indistinguishable by label.
   - Performance team's canonical ToC is actually the *service* ToC (01/05) — team has no distinct one.
   - Handbook nav adjacency implies it's *inside* Organization when URLs are siblings.
3. **Top 3 low-risk high-impact fixes:**
   - Add a one-line scope badge on each of the three "performance" pages ("This is the TEAM / the SERVICE / the PROGRAM").
   - Retitle the team ToC entry so it reads as the team's, or explicitly note it doubles as the service ToC.
   - Flatten or visually separate Handbook from Organization in the nav.
4. **Naming issue:** "Performance Management Theory of Change" under the *Performance team* — service title in a team slot.
5. **Drift risk:** ToC summaries page claims "single source of truth, one place to maintain," but five service ToCs live under `/our-teams/` while the index lives under `/resources/` — easy for one to fork from the other over time.
6. **Audience miss:** an agency Data-Driven Officer who only needs "what's my next Stat, what's the pre-memo standard, who's my contact" has no single landing — they're routed through OPI's internal taxonomy.
7. **Should be canonical (isn't):** the **CitiStat Method Playbook** — it's the de facto staff bible for running Stats but is filed as one card among six under the program. Mark it the canonical operating reference.
