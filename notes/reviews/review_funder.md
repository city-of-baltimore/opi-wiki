# Funder / Council Staffer Review — OPI Foundations

**Lens:** Does money map to outcomes for residents, and can oversight find a budgeted service without a tour guide?

## Scenario findings

**1. Find the five services.** Yes — cleanly. `how-we-work/services/` opens with a one-line frame ("five services, delivered by four teams") and a three-column table: Service / What OPI delivers / Delivered by. AdminOps, Citywide Performance Management, Citywide Data & Analytics, Innovation Lab, Cross-Agency Delivery. This is the most legible page on the site. The "delivered by" column is exactly what an appropriator wants.

**2. CitiStat → resident outcomes.** Traceable but I had to assemble it. `programs/citistat/` correctly frames CitiStat as a *program, not a team*, supported by all teams, with the signal-to-solution loop named. The outcomes language ("faster service, fewer errors, better resident experience") lives one click away on the Theory of Change Summaries index, which routes to five per-service TOCs. The chain holds; it just isn't on one canonical page a Council member would land on cold.

**3. Public-facing work.** Present: `products/` lists the Baltimore Intelligence Center (Active) plus two Placeholders, with a Status column — honest. `public/` holds plain-language CitiStat / Data / Innovation Lab briefs and the website copy/IA masters. Good accountability surface.

**4. Budgeted service buried? The real flag.** Cross-Agency Delivery itself is handled *well* — full dedicated page, its own Theory of Change, parity in the services table, explicit "service overlay, not a cost center... activated through Innovation Lab and Performance, no standing team." That is the right disclosure. **But the entire `services/` section is buried one level inside the "How We Work" tab.** The top nav exposes *Our Teams*, *Programs*, and *Products* as tabs — but not *Services*. The five budgeted deliverables are a sub-item under a process label, while the org chart ("Our Teams") gets a front-row tab. From an oversight seat, that inverts the priority: teams are visible, the services taxpayers fund are a drill-down.

**5. Does `products/` belong top-level?** Yes. Products are the public-facing, resident-touching outputs (BIC, data platform, performance portal). For visibility and accountability they should stay discoverable at the top. Keep it.

**6. IA: collapse Teams + Programs + Products under "Our Work"?** **Hurts.** A single "Our Work" parent would push Products *down* a level and still leave Services missing from the top — the opposite of what oversight needs. If anything, promote **Services** to a top tab. The fix is not fewer top items; it's the *right* top items: Services and Products forward, internal team/program structure secondary.

---

## Forced outputs

1. **Reaction:** Agree-with-changes — content is production-ready; the nav hierarchy is not.
2. **Top 3 blockers:** (a) "Services" has no top-level nav entry — budgeted deliverables are buried under "How We Work." (b) No single canonical "what OPI delivers and the outcomes it buys" landing page for a cold Council reader. (c) Home hero cards (about-us, CitiStat method, glossary, Director letters) offer no path to Services or Products.
3. **Top 3 low-risk fixes:** (a) Promote `services/` to a top-level tab (or alias it). (b) Add a home card "What OPI delivers — five services" → services index. (c) Add a Status/owner line to each product placeholder so empty pages read as roadmap, not rot.
4. **Naming issue:** "Innovation Lab" is simultaneously a team, a service, AND a product-builder. The page flags this as intentional, but for a funder it muddies the service-vs-team-vs-cost-center line every appropriation depends on.
5. **Drift risk:** Five separate per-service Theories of Change with no enforced template parity will diverge in rigor over time; the index can't hold them to one logic on its own.
6. **Audience need missed:** A budget-to-outcome crosswalk — which budgeted service produces which resident outcome — exists in prose but never as one scannable artifact.
7. **Should be canonical:** `how-we-work/services/` is the de facto money-maps-to-mission page but is treated as a subsection. Make it canonical and link every team/program/product back to it.

**Verdict — keep `products/` top-level? YES.** Products are OPI's resident-facing output and its clearest accountability surface; demoting them under an "Our Work" umbrella would reduce public visibility. The structural problem is the missing top-level *Services* tab, not the presence of *Products*.
