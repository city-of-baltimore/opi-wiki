## 4. Service pages — section-level consistency (the five siblings)

The five pages (`citywide-performance-management`, `citywide-data-and-analytics`, `innovation-lab`, `cross-agency-delivery`, `admin-ops`) share a template: *What this service does / The goal / Mandate / Priority outcomes / What this means for people / Delivered by / Read alongside*. Cross-Agency Delivery adds a "How to request this service" section.

**Parallelism findings (drift to flag):**

1. **"What this service does" opener drifts in shape.** Three pages open "This service [verb]s…" (performance: "administers"; data: "strengthens"; innovation: "operates OPI's Innovation Lab, a…"; cross-agency: "coordinates"). AdminOps breaks the pattern: "This service — OPI's Administration & Operations function — is responsible for the administrative, operational…" — passive ("is responsible for") and em-dash-interrupted where the others lead with an active verb. **[judgment]** Recommend AdminOps adopt the sibling shape: "This service runs OPI's administrative, operational, communications, and portfolio-management functions…" (see §9).

2. **"What this service does" length is uneven.** Performance, Data, Innovation, and AdminOps each = one framing sentence + one bolded "**Activities include**…" list-sentence. Cross-Agency Delivery is markedly longer: framing sentence + a long "coordination layer" sentence + the Activities sentence + a third paragraph on the three activation patterns. It reads as a heavier sibling. That is defensible (it is the most structurally unusual service), but flag that it breaks visual parity. **[judgment]**

3. **"The goal" is consistently strong and parallel** across all five (each is one sentence, active, "Help/Make/Keep…"). No drift. Good.

4. **"Mandate" parallel but formats vary.** Performance and Data cite a clean "Baltimore City Code, Article 1, Subtitle NN (Name)." Innovation cites "Subtitle 61 (Innovation Lab / continuous improvement)." Cross-Agency and AdminOps use prose ("Operationally required to execute…" / "Supported by CitiStat commitments and…"). The prose ones are necessary because those services lack a standalone mandate — acceptable, but the tonal shift is worth a deliberate eye. No forced change. **[judgment — no action required]**

5. **"Priority outcomes" — parallel and clean** across all five (4 bullets each, declarative). On-target. The one nit: Performance's bullet 3 ("Leadership can see service performance without digging through spreadsheets") and Data's bullet ("decisions are based on trusted, current numbers") both reappear nearly verbatim in their "What this means for people" sections. Intentional echo; acceptable.

6. **"What this means for people" — strong and parallel** (4 first-person persona bullets each). This is the most human, most on-brand section across all five. No drift.

7. **"Delivered by" length varies by necessity.** AdminOps and Data are one line; Performance, Innovation, and Cross-Agency are longer because delivery is shared. Defensible.

**Verdict on parity:** Four of five read as tight siblings. AdminOps's opener is the one true voice-drift (passive + em-dash where siblings are active). Cross-Agency Delivery is the length outlier but for structural reasons.

Meanwhile the `index.md` "Squad at a glance" lists six people but not Gabriel Watson.
