# Review 2 — Funder / Council Staffer Lens

**Reviewer persona:** Funder / City Council oversight staff. I track budgeted services and want money mapping to resident outcomes.

## Scenario findings

**1. The five services.** Clearly enumerated at `/what-we-do/services/`: Citywide Performance Management, Citywide Data and Analytics, Innovation Lab, Cross-Agency Delivery, AdminOps. The table pairs each with "what OPI delivers" and "delivered by." This is the single biggest Round 1 fix — Services are now front-and-center under What We Do, not buried. Legible. The service/team/program/product distinctions are stated plainly.

**2. CitiStat → outcomes.** Traceable. The crosswalk (`/resources/reference/service-crosswalk/`) ties Performance Management to **Article 1, Subtitle 61 (CitiStat)** → goal ("address problems before they become chronic") → outcomes ("problems addressed before chronic; commitments kept with evidence"). CitiStat is correctly framed as the *program* the Performance team runs, with a deep portfolio (19 agency briefs). Mandate→goal→outcomes lands on one page. Strong.

**3. Public-facing work.** Products page lists the Baltimore Intelligence Center (Active) plus two placeholders. One-pager names concrete public commitments: 311 modernization, Accela permit dashboard "live," published AI policy. Evidence exists but the public *outputs* are mostly forward-looking or placeholder.

**4. Buried budgeted services.** Cross-Agency Delivery is **well-served**, not buried — full service page with named outcomes, governance, and an honest "no standalone code mandate; operationally required" framing. Good. The real exposure: it has no standing team or line budget, which oversight will ask about. Document that explicitly.

**5. Grouping judgment.** What We Do (Services + Programs + Products) beats the old buried-Services structure decisively. A reviewer can now answer "what does OPI deliver?" in one click.

## The budget-to-outcome test

The crosswalk gives me **mandate → goal → outcomes** per service — excellent. But it has **no dollars**. The one-pager has **one top-line figure ($2.03M FY26)** and no per-service allocation. So the chain breaks: I cannot see what each service *costs* next to what it *buys*. That's two pages, and neither closes the loop.

---

## Forced outputs

1. **Reaction:** Agree-with-changes. Structurally production-ready; the accountability spine has one missing vertebra (per-service cost).
2. **Top 3 blockers:** (a) No per-service budget — top-line $2.03M can't be allocated to the five services or three FY26 priorities. (b) Crosswalk lives under Resources/Reference, not linked prominently from the budget hearing audience's entry point. (c) Two of three products are placeholders, so "public value" reads aspirational.
3. **Top 3 low-risk fixes:** (a) Add a cost-center / FTE column to the crosswalk (even ranges). (b) Cross-link one-pager ↔ crosswalk bidirectionally. (c) Date-stamp the products page with each placeholder's expected ship quarter.
4. **Naming issue:** "AdminOps" is internal jargon for an oversight audience; "Director's Office Operations" or "Agency Operations" reads better in a budget hearing.
5. **Drift risk:** The $2.03M figure and "19 Stats by Jan 2026" live only on the one-pager (v1.0, April 2026). As budget cycles turn, this dates fast with no per-service anchor to reconcile against.
6. **Audience need missed:** No per-service or per-priority *spend-to-outcome* line — the core funder/Council artifact. The crosswalk implies it ("cost center") but never shows a number.
7. **Should be canonical:** The **Service Crosswalk** should be the canonical budget-accountability page (promoted out of Resources/Reference into Services), with the one-pager's dollars merged in.

**Does the Service Crosswalk satisfy "budget-to-outcome on one page"? Partly.** Mandate→goal→outcomes: yes, cleanly. Budget→outcome: no — the dollars live on a different page and aren't broken out per service.
