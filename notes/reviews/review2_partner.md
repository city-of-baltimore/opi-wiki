# Review 2 — Partner Agency (Deputy Director POV)

*Persona: deputy director at another Baltimore City agency. Just got a CitiStat follow-up request; have a messy multi-agency problem. Five-minute self-serve test.*

## Scenario walkthrough

**1. CitiStat follow-up request.** Home → What We Do → How to Engage OPI lands me on a page openly labeled "FOR PARTNERS." The "What to do with a CitiStat request" section is exactly what I needed: a follow-up means a Stat commitment is being tracked to closure, and **my agency's Data-Driven Officer coordinates the response.** Clear, fast, correct. The one gap: it tells me *who* (my DDO) but not *what's expected of me by when* — it punts to the CitiStat program page, which is staff-oriented and makes me hunt. Good enough in five minutes, but a deputy director wants the deliverable up front.

**2. Multi-agency problem — how to get help AND request it.** The "How to request a service" table routes "a stalled problem that spans multiple agencies" to Cross-Agency Delivery, with the crucial caveat right there: activations need a sponsoring Authorizer at Deputy Mayor / City Administrator level, and OPI helps me frame the request. Contact is a real email plus "raise it through your CitiStat process." Genuinely actionable — I know who initiates (me, via my sponsor) and where to start.

**3. Cross-Agency Delivery service page.** Reads well for an outsider. "When problems span multiple agencies or get stuck for months, we step in to help unblock them" — plain. "How to request this service" repeats the sponsor requirement and links back to How to Engage. The "What this means for people" personas (resident, director, leader, frontline) sell it without jargon. Minor: "coordination overlay," "commitment ledger," "Senior Responsible Owners" arrive before they're defined — insider vocabulary in an outsider-facing page.

**4. Team vs service vs program — after 5 min?** Yes, now I can state it. The Services hub says it plainly: teams have staff and budget; services are what teams deliver; CitiStat is a program (a routine). Three hubs agree. The removed "both a team and a service" callout is not missed. The remaining snag: **the "Innovation Lab" service is delivered by the "Innovation Lab team"** — same name for a team and a service, which momentarily undercuts the very distinction the page is teaching.

## Forced outputs

**1. One-line reaction:** Agree-with-changes — close to production-ready; outsiders can self-serve, a few insider-vocabulary and canonical-page gaps remain.

**2. Top 3 blockers:**
- CitiStat follow-up page tells me *who coordinates* but not *what I owe or by when* — forces a hop to a staff page.
- "Innovation Lab" naming collision (team = service) quietly breaks the taxonomy lesson.
- Cross-Agency Delivery page front-loads undefined terms (coordination overlay, commitment ledger, SRO) before defining them.

**3. Top 3 low-risk high-impact fixes:**
- Add a one-line "what's expected of your agency" + typical turnaround to the CitiStat-request section.
- Gloss "Data-Driven Officer" inline on first use (one clause: "your agency's designated CitiStat liaison").
- Add a plain-language gloss for "coordination overlay" / "commitment ledger" on the CAD service page.

**4. Naming/nomenclature issue:** The service named "Innovation Lab" is delivered by the "Innovation Lab team" — identical labels for two taxonomy types on the same page. Consider renaming the *service* (e.g., "Service Design & Innovation") to keep team ≠ service legible.

**5. Drift risk:** The "x-agency delivery" short form is permitted on the service page but index.md/contributing only emphasize "never CAD, spell it out." As pages multiply, the approved short form will quietly diverge — pin it in the style guide.

**6. Audience need the structure misses:** No "respond to a CitiStat request" *checklist/template* for partner agencies — the page names the owner but offers no artifact a deputy director can hand to staff.

**7. Page that should be canonical (and isn't):** "How to Engage OPI" is the true partner front door but lives three clicks deep under What We Do. It deserves a top-level or homepage "Partner agency? Start here" anchor.

**Does the Cross-Agency Delivery page read clearly to an outsider now?** **Partly.** The what/why/how-to-request and personas are genuinely clear and actionable; requesting it is now self-serve. It loses a point only on undefined governance vocabulary that lands before it's explained.
