# Review 2 — Storyteller / Brand Strategist

Persona: communications & brand strategist. Test: one crisp story, one central metaphor, zero jargon drift.

## 1. OPI in one sentence (from the site)
"OPI runs Baltimore City's performance and innovation work as a signal-to-solution loop — CitiStat spots the problem, Data explains it, the Innovation Lab designs and tests the fix, Cross-Agency Delivery ships it across agencies, and AdminOps sustains the gain." That is genuinely repeatable. Round 1's biggest win: the office now *has* a one-liner.

## 2. Is the loop a strong front door, and is it told consistently?
The metaphor is strong — it is the best brand asset on the site and deserves to be the front door. Consistency is **improved but not fixed.** Round 1 flagged 3–5 wordings plus a verbatim dupe. Now most pages correctly *link* to the canonical How Work Moves page (our-teams, what-we-do/index, citistat) — good discipline. But the full sentence is still re-spelled in at least five places with drift:

- **Home:** "Data and Analytics explains it ... AdminOps sustains it."
- **How We Work index:** "Data explains it ... AdminOps sustains it" (numbered list).
- **how-work-moves (canonical):** "Data helps explain it ... keeps the work visible, documented, and sustained."
- **teams-programs-foundations:** TWO different blockquotes on one page — "Data helps explain it ... documented, and sustained" AND "Data and Analytics helps explain them ... documented, and accountable." (visible/documented/**accountable** is a third tail.)
- **operating-frame:** a *fifth* retelling as a 5-row "How the cycle moves" table with different step names (See it / Understand it / Decide and commit / Build and ship / Sustain and tell).

So the verb tail mutates — "sustains it" vs "keeps the work visible, documented, and sustained" vs "...and accountable" — and the verbs for Data drift ("explains" / "helps explain" / "explains them"). Same story, five costumes. Pick ONE canonical sentence, stamp it on how-work-moves, and have every other page link to it rather than paraphrase.

## 3. Jargon / acronym audit
- **"CAD" is clean.** The only literal "CAD" instances mean Computer-Aided Dispatch (BIC roadmap + governance pages) — valid. Every Cross-Agency-Delivery context spells it out and three pages explicitly forbid the abbreviation. Pass.
- **New acronym table in the glossary is a real upgrade** — BoB, DCA, DDO, GIS, PII, PIN, RAG, TPM, plus the "VPN/SSO/MFA carry common meanings" line. Exactly the right move.
- **Remaining jargon risk:** the office speaks two dialects. "Service" appears as both a count of 5 (Operating Frame, glossary) and the taxonomy table calls them services; meanwhile **"portfolio" = "team"** and "Citywide Performance Management" = "Performance." A newcomer meets four words for the same boxes (team/portfolio, and service-named-twice). Also "AdminOps" never appears in the taxonomy table as a team but is a service — fine, but unexplained on Home.

## 4. Same idea told twice (drift / redundancy)
- **Two legacy operating-model pages survive:** Operating Frame and Teams-Programs-Foundations both sit under How We Work and both re-explain the whole model — one via "five services + cycle table," the other via "four portfolios + foundations." They overlap each other AND the canonical page. This is the core redundancy: three pages competing to be the operating model.
- **The taxonomy** (teams/services/programs/products) is defined on Home, again in What We Do, and again term-by-term in the glossary — acceptable as reference, but the Home table and glossary should agree on whether AdminOps is listed.

---

## Forced outputs
1. **One-line reaction:** Agree-with-changes. The story is finally memorable; redundancy and a drifting tail keep it from production-ready.
2. **Top 3 blockers:** (a) Two legacy operating-model pages (Operating Frame + Teams-Programs-Foundations) duplicate each other and the canonical page; merge or demote. (b) The loop sentence has 5 wordings with a mutating verb tail. (c) Four words for the same units (team/portfolio; service named twice) — pick one public dialect.
3. **Top 3 low-risk high-impact fixes:** (a) Lock ONE canonical loop sentence on how-work-moves; replace every other full retelling with a link. (b) Add AdminOps to the Home taxonomy table or footnote why it's absent. (c) Standardize Data's verb to "explains it" everywhere.
4. **Naming issue:** "Portfolio" and "Team" are used interchangeably for the same four units — costly for partners; pick "Team" publicly, retire "portfolio" to internal docs.
5. **Drift risk:** the loop's closing clause ("sustains it" / "documented and sustained" / "documented and accountable") — three tails will keep diverging without a single source.
6. **Audience need missed:** a resident/press reader needs a 30-second "what OPI does for me" framing; the loop is told in OPI's internal vocabulary, not outcomes residents feel.
7. **Should be canonical (and isn't):** How Work Moves Through OPI is named canonical but Operating Frame and Teams-Programs-Foundations still compete with it — fold both into it so it is the single operating-model page.
