# Partner Review — Deputy Director, peer Baltimore City agency

I came in cold with two real needs: a CitiStat follow-up request just landed on me, and I have a messy problem touching DHCD, DOT, and DPW that I want OPI help with.

## Scenario walkthrough

**1. What happens after a CitiStat follow-up request?** From the homepage I clicked Programs → CitiStat. The index is honest but routes me wrong: the "Public reader" lane sends me to the Strategic Framework, not to "what a follow-up is and what's expected of me." The actual answer (T+2 follow-up memo, commitment tracker, "relentless follow-up until closed with evidence") lives buried in the **Method Playbook**, which the page explicitly frames as "OPI staff" material. As the agency person *receiving* the request, I'm reading a doc that wasn't written for me. I got the answer in ~6 minutes, but only by ignoring the signage.

**2. Help with a multi-agency problem.** This worked. Homepage "How work moves through OPI" → Cross-Agency Delivery. The line "Permitting cuts across DHCD, DOT, and Public Works" was the moment I knew I was in the right place. Good.

**3. Cross-Agency Delivery page, in my words:** OPI doesn't stand up a new agency to fix cross-cutting problems; it drops a temporary "coordination overlay" on top of the agencies that already own the work — naming an Authorizer (a Deputy Mayor / City Administrator), a Senior Responsible Owner, decision rights, a commitment ledger, and a weekly cadence — then trains an owner and exits. It activates three ways: Tiger Team, standing delivery review, embedded delivery. **Does authority come through? Partly.** I learn the *Authorizer is* a Deputy Mayor/CA, but not how *I* trigger this. There's no intake path, no "email opi@ / file a request" — for a service whose whole point is convening agencies, the page never tells an outside agency how to ask to be convened.

**4. Team vs service vs program:** I can state it. Team = staff + budget (Performance, Data, Innovation Lab, Director's Office). Service = what OPI delivers (the five). Program = standing routine spanning teams (CitiStat). The homepage table plus the Services intro made this land. The one that wobbles: Innovation Lab is both a team and a service — flagged as "intentional," which I accept but had to re-read.

## Forced outputs

1. **Reaction:** agree-with-changes. Structurally sound, but outsider entry points are missing.
2. **Top 3 blockers:** (a) No intake / "how do I ask for this" path on Cross-Agency Delivery or CitiStat — the two pages an outsider actually arrives at; (b) CitiStat follow-up answer is shelved under staff-only Method Playbook with no partner-facing version; (c) Authority is described but not actionable — I know *who* authorizes, not *how a request reaches them*.
3. **Top 3 low-risk fixes:** (a) Add a "Partner / agency receiving a request" lane to the CitiStat index linking the follow-up expectations directly; (b) Add a one-line "How to request this service" + opi@baltimorecity.gov to the CAD page; (c) On the CAD page, add a sentence on who can *initiate* (can a deputy director like me ask, or only a Deputy Mayor?).
4. **Naming issue:** "Cross-Agency Delivery is a service overlay, not a standing team and not a cost center." "Overlay" and "cost center" are insider terms; an outsider doesn't parse them. Say "a temporary coordination effort, not a permanent office."
5. **Drift risk:** The signal-to-solution loop is restated verbatim on home, How-Work-Moves, Services, CitiStat, and CAD. Five copies will diverge. Make How-Work-Moves canonical and have the rest transclude or link.
6. **Audience miss:** The structure assumes the reader is OPI staff or "the public." The *partner agency being asked to act* — me — has no home. That's the most common external reader.
7. **Should be canonical:** "How Work Moves Through OPI" is the real map and should be the single source for the loop and the team/service/program distinction; today the homepage and Services page duplicate it instead of deferring to it.

**Verdict on the Cross-Agency Delivery page — Partly.** It reads clearly as a *description* and the cross-cutting framing is excellent, but it fails the outsider's actual test: cross-agency authority is *named* (who authorizes) without being *actionable* (how I, a deputy director, request convening). Clear explainer, missing front door.
